# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-06-05)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-94/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-94/daily-digest)

On June 3, 2026, the Senate voted 53-46 to proceed with consideration of S. 2, the Secure America Act, a reconciliation bill focused on immigration enforcement funding for ICE and Customs and Border Protection, while the House voted on multiple bills addressing government accountability and foreign policy. The House passed measures under suspension of rules concerning the American Battlefield Protection Program, Alaska Native ivory sales, and marine conservation, and agreed to concurrent resolutions directing the President to remove armed forces from hostilities with Iran and Lebanon. Additionally, the House advanced legislation on welfare program fraud prevention and child care compliance, though Democrats raised concerns about executive overreach and the administration's use of federal funding as political leverage.

The House debated H.R. 8872, the Preventing Waste, Fraud, and Abuse in TANF Act, which seeks to strengthen oversight of the Temporary Assistance for Needy Families program by requiring states to report improper payments, establish spending deadlines for accumulated funds, and prevent misuse of federal resources. Republicans argued the measure protects vulnerable families by ensuring taxpayer dollars reach intended beneficiaries rather than sitting unused or being diverted to unrelated state projects, citing Government Accountability Office findings that states hold nearly $8 billion in unspent TANF funds. Democrats countered that the bill grants the Trump administration expansive authority to withhold assistance under vague fraud definitions, potentially weaponizing the program after the administration had already frozen $10 billion in social services funding to blue states, and contended the legislation fails to address actual sources of TANF fraud.

The House also passed the Stop Child Care Scams Act of 2026, a comprehensive package designed to strengthen program integrity in federal child care assistance by requiring states to measure and report improper payments, lowering the improper payment threshold from ten to five percent, implementing cyclical monitoring at three-year intervals, and permanently debarring fraudulent providers from receiving federal funds. Supporters cited cases like the Minnesota Feeding Our Future scheme, which cost taxpayers over $250 million, as justification for stronger oversight.
