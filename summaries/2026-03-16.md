# Congressional Digest Summary (March 12, 2026)

## Senate Actions

The Senate passed the Housing for the 21st Century Act (H.R. 6644) with strong bipartisan support, voting 89-10 to increase housing supply across America. The chamber also unanimously approved a resolution celebrating the YMCA's 175th anniversary of service to American communities.

## Legislative Stalls and Committee Votes

The Senate rejected a motion to proceed to the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act, with a 51-46 vote falling short of the required 60 votes for cloture. Senate Majority Leader Thune entered a motion to reconsider this procedural vote.

## Judicial Nomination Advancing

The Senate advanced consideration of Anna St. John's nomination to be U.S. District Judge for the Eastern District of Louisiana. A cloture vote on the nomination is scheduled for 5:30 p.m. on Monday, March 16, 2026, with floor debate resuming at 3 p.m. that day.

## Senate Committee Activity

Multiple Senate committees held hearings and business meetings, including the Armed Services Committee examining U.S. European Command and Transportation Command posture, the Commerce Committee favorably reporting five nominations for positions at NASA, NTSB, and the Transportation Department, and the Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions Committee hearing on foreign influence in higher education.

## House Legislative Output

The House introduced 44 public bills and 6 resolutions, while three committee reports were filed on immigration enforcement, giant sequoia conservation, and establishing the Chiricahua National Park in Arizona. The House met briefly at 9:30 a.m. for a prayer and journal approval before adjourning at 9:38 a.m.

## New Legislation Introduced

Senators introduced 29 bills addressing diverse issues including national service expansion (ACTION Act calling for 1 million service positions within a decade), cryptocurrency prohibitions for Social Security investments, and accountability measures related to civilian casualties in Gaza conflicts. House bills covered education fraud prevention, higher education reforms, and healthcare access improvements.
