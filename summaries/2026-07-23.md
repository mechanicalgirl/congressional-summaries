# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-07-23)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-119/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-119/daily-digest)

On July 21, 2026, both chambers of Congress conducted extensive legislative business. The Senate passed the Affordable Prescriptions For Patients Act addressing patent infringement for biological products, authorized use of the Capitol Rotunda for a ceremony honoring late Senator Lindsey Graham, and achieved a unanimous-consent agreement on judicial nominations including votes for federal judge nominees. The House continued significant debate and voting on the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2027, considering over 100 amendments addressing military personnel policies, defense procurement, weapons systems, and strategic initiatives. The House also passed the Main Street Capital Access Act improving federal banking laws and a continuing appropriations measure for fiscal year 2027, both by narrow margins.

The House spent considerable time debating amendments to the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2027, which would authorize $1.15 trillion in discretionary defense spending. Chairman Mike Rogers emphasized that the bill aims to revitalize the defense industrial base, noting that the number of prime defense contractors has declined from 51 in the 1990s to just six, while China has expanded dramatically and controls over 90 percent of critical mineral processing. Ranking Member Adam Smith acknowledged bipartisan development but expressed concerns about the partisan amendment rule, the ongoing costly war with Iran lacking clear strategy, and the overall fiscal responsibility of combining $1.15 trillion in defense spending with substantial tax cuts and domestic program reductions.

The defense authorization legislation contained extensive provisions across multiple areas including military procurement, personnel policies, family support services, and supply chain security. Key sections established multiyear procurement authorities for F-15EX and F-35 aircraft, authorized military end strengths totaling over 1.3 million active duty personnel across service branches, reformed military personnel practices to base all actions on individual merit, and established new support services for military families including expanded child care assistance and relocation accommodations. The bill also created requirements for comprehensive research into military aviator neurological health risks from flight exposure and established an Intellectual Property Ombudsman to resolve disputes between contractors and the Department of Defense.

The legislation included significant provisions addressing defense industrial base vulnerabilities and supply chain security.
