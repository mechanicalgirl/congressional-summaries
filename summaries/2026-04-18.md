# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-04-18)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-67/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-67/daily-digest)

The House of Representatives passed legislation on April 16, 2026, addressing environmental regulations, immigration policy, and surveillance authorities. The chamber approved amendments to the Clean Air Act and EPA review processes by narrow margins of approximately 220-205 votes, and voted 224-204 to grant temporary protected status to Haiti through 2029. The House also passed legislation extending Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act authorities through April 30, 2026, after a failed procedural vote on related amendments, and rejected a War Powers Resolution directing the President to withdraw from hostilities with Iran by a single vote, falling 213-214.

The debate over Haiti's temporary protected status divided along partisan lines, with Democrats emphasizing that the country faces severe humanitarian conditions with armed gangs controlling 90 percent of the capital and displacing over 1.4 million people. They argued that approximately 350,000 Haitian TPS holders already in the United States are legally vetted, employed in critical sectors, and contribute billions annually to the economy. Republicans contended that roughly 91 percent of these Haitians entered illegally rather than as stranded visitors the program was designed to protect, and pointed to crimes committed by Haitian nationals on TPS status. The bill ultimately passed through a bipartisan discharge petition after facing an uncertain path in the Senate and potential Supreme Court intervention.

The House debate on the Iran War Powers Resolution revealed sharp partisan divisions, with Democrats arguing the President launched an illegal war without congressional authorization that resulted in deaths of American servicemembers and thousands of civilians while disrupting global trade and energy markets. Republicans defended the military operation as responding to Iran's decades-long hostility and attacks on U.S. forces and merchant vessels, arguing it methodically degraded Iranian military capabilities. The resolution failed by one vote at 213-214, falling short of passage.

She expressed frustration over stalled negotiations on Department of Homeland Security funding over ICE and Border Patrol reforms, describing it as the longest DHS shutdown in history.
