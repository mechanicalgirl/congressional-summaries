# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-04-08)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-62/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-62/daily-digest)

On Monday, April 6, 2026, both chambers of Congress held pro forma sessions with minimal activity, as the Senate met for one minute at 1:02 p.m. and the House convened for two minutes at 10 a.m. During this abbreviated legislative day, the House introduced 15 public bills numbered H.R. 8196-8210 and 2 resolutions while filing eight reports focused on amendments to the Child Care and Development Block Grant Act of 1990, addressing fraud prevention, improper payment thresholds, state performance reviews, and sanctions against noncompliant providers. The chambers also processed executive communications from federal agencies including final rules from the Federal Housing Finance Agency, Environmental Protection Agency, and Nuclear Regulatory Commission, along with No FEAR Act compliance reports from various government departments.

The Congressional Record further documented Extensions of Remarks honoring several notable constituents and organizations, including the induction of Dr. Jack Sternberg to the Arkansas Walk of Fame, the College of Marin's centennial celebration, Dr. Rito Silva's 35-year tenure in educational leadership, Connor Hill's victory in the Regeneron Science Talent Search, and Staff Sergeant Phillip Cook's recognition with the French Legion of Honor. Both chambers are scheduled to reconvene on Thursday, April 9, 2026, for additional pro forma sessions.
