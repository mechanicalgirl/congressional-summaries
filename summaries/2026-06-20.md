# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-06-20)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-103/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-103/daily-digest)

The Senate spent Thursday, June 18, 2026, advancing the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act and scheduling votes on housing supply legislation, while also confirming George Holding as U.S. Director of the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development and numerous military nominees. The chamber passed the Declaration of Independence Reaffirmation Act and the MAP for Broadband Funding Act to enhance broadband deployment efficiency. Meanwhile, the House held a brief pro forma session during which four public bills and seven resolutions were introduced on topics including rescue dog adoption pilots, artificial intelligence initiatives, education, healthcare, and financial services.

Numerous amendments to the fiscal year 2027 defense authorization bill addressed diverse national security and economic priorities. Senator McCormick introduced amendments on Russian petroleum sanctions with nuanced exceptions, Ready Reserve Force vessel modernization, and comprehensive assessments of unmanned systems command and control architectures. Additional proposals established a National Commission on American Leadership in Robotics to examine competitiveness and supply chain resilience, a Veterans Affairs grant program for traumatic brain injury treatment, and various technical matters including antivenom banking and environmental review procedures. Amendments also expanded Ukraine support fund uses, modified cemetery interment eligibility, restricted Department of Defense information technology procurement sources, and established a Blue Skies for Taiwan working group to develop secure drone production capacity independent of Chinese components.

The National Quantum Initiative Reauthorization Act of 2026 substantially expands the federal government's quantum information science program through comprehensive amendments broadening research and development scope. Key provisions authorize the National Institute of Standards and Technology to establish up to three quantum centers with eighteen million dollars annual funding per center, expand the National Science Foundation's educational initiatives including traineeships and fellowships, and require establishment of quantum testbeds. The amendment creates new international cooperation requirements, mandates post-quantum cryptography deployment strategies across federal agencies, establishes supply chain security measures, and creates a new Title V establishing NASA's quantum research role related to space and aeronautics applications.

Senator Kaine criticized the Trump administration's recent memorandum of understanding with Iran, comparing it unfavorably to the 2015 Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action. Kaine detailed how the new agreement weakens nuclear development protections, eliminates uranium enrichment and centrifuge limitations, removes inspection guarantees, and surrenders leverage on non-nuclear sanctions
