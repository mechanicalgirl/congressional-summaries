# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-07-22)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-118/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-118/daily-digest)

The Senate convened on July 20, 2026, to advance multiple judicial nominations through post-cloture procedures, including appointments to circuit and district courts. The chamber reached a unanimous consent agreement on Benjamin M. Flowers's nomination for the Sixth Circuit, scheduling continued consideration for the following day. Meanwhile, the House completed action on fourteen healthcare and administrative bills, including reauthorizations for maternal health, traumatic brain injury, and diabetes programs, while advancing major legislative items such as the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2027 and the Stop Insider Trading Act for floor consideration.

During this legislative session, Congress received 186 executive communications from federal agencies regarding new rules and policy decisions. These included final rules from the National Credit Union Administration on records preservation, Federal Trade Commission guidance on negative options and non-compete clauses, and numerous Environmental Protection Agency determinations on air quality standards. Multiple agencies submitted rules addressing civil rights compliance, the Federal Railroad Administration transmitted regulations on safety and reporting procedures, and the Internal Revenue Service provided guidance on qualified trusts and information reporting requirements.

The House passed several bills focused on government transparency and procurement efficiency. The Stop Secret Spending Act requires federal agencies to report other transaction agreements to USAspending.gov, closing a significant transparency gap that previously allowed nearly twenty billion dollars in annual federal spending to go unreported. The Ensuring Federal Purchasing Efficiency Act modified acquisition threshold adjustments from every five years to every three years, while the Value Over Cost Act permits the General Services Administration to award contracts based on best value rather than lowest cost.

The House also advanced multiple public health bills with bipartisan support, including measures to address medical provider fraud in workers' compensation programs and reauthorize research initiatives on maternal health disparities, traumatic brain injury, dementia care, and vector-borne disease prevention. The FDA Modernization Act 3.0 updated regulatory language regarding testing methods, and the Destruction of Hazardous Imports Act granted the FDA authority to compel destruction of contaminated or dangerous imports at importer expense.

Senate debate on July 20 centered on election security and judicial nominations. Republicans introduced the SAVE America Act, requiring voter identification and proof of citizenship for federal elections, while Democrats opposed the measure, citing limited documented cases of noncitizen voting and characterizing citizenship documentation requirements as potentially discriminatory.
