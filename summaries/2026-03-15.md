# Congressional Record Summary - March 12, 2026

## Senate Activity

The Senate passed the Housing for the 21st Century Act (H.R. 6644) by a vote of 89-10, legislation designed to increase housing supply across America. The chamber also approved a resolution celebrating the 175th anniversary of the YMCA, recognizing its contributions to communities nationwide.

## Supreme Court Nomination Controversy

The Senate began consideration of Anna St. John's nomination as United States District Judge for the Eastern District of Louisiana. A cloture vote is scheduled for Monday, March 16 at 5:30 p.m., with debate continuing until that time. This nomination represents an ongoing confirmation process in the judiciary branch.

## Failed Appropriations Cloture Vote

The Senate rejected a cloture motion on the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act (H.R. 7147) by a vote of 51-46, falling short of the 60-vote threshold needed to advance the measure. Senator Thune subsequently filed a motion to reconsider this vote.

## Nominations and Withdrawals

The Senate received nomination of Sarah B. Rogers of New York for Chief Executive Officer of the United States Agency for Global Media, and one Army nomination for major general. However, two previous nominations were withdrawn: Jeremy Carl for Assistant Secretary of State and William Hewes III for Consumer Product Safety Commission.

## House Legislative Activity

The House introduced 44 public bills (H.R. 7890-7933) and 6 resolutions during the session. Three bills received committee reports: legislation on immigration enforcement (H.R. 7640), giant sequoia health and resiliency (H.R. 2709), and establishment of Chiricahua National Park in Arizona (H.R. 6380).

## House Committee Hearings

Senate committees held several significant hearings. The Armed Services Committee examined U.S. European Command and Transportation Command posture for fiscal year 2027 defense authorization. The Commerce Committee advanced multiple nominations including appointments to NASA, the National Transportation Safety Board, and Transportation Department positions.
