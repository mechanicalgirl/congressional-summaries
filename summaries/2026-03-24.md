# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-03-24)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-53/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-53/daily-digest)

The Senate convened on Sunday, March 22, 2026, and passed four bills addressing environmental and economic priorities. The FISH Act (S. 688) was approved with an amendment establishing an IUU vessel list to combat illegal, unreported, and unregulated fishing globally, including visa sanctions for owners of vessels engaged in such activities and provisions addressing forced labor in fishing operations. The Senate also passed the Sea Turtle Rescue Assistance and Rehabilitation Act (S. 843), the WIPPES Act (S. 1092) requiring "Do Not Flush" labeling on disposable wipes, and the Global Investment in American Jobs Act (S. 2563), which directs the Commerce Department to study ways to increase U.S. competitiveness in attracting foreign direct investment from trusted countries. Additionally, two resolutions were adopted: one designating March 21, 2026, as National Day of Play to promote social connection and reduce device usage, and another recognizing the third week of March 2026 as National CACFP Week to honor the Child and Adult Care Food Program's role in providing nutrition to vulnerable children and adults.

The Senate continued consideration of the Markwayne Mullin nomination for Secretary of Homeland Security, invoking cloture on March 22 with a vote of 54-37. Scheduled for Monday, March 23 at approximately 7:45 p.m., the Senate will hold a final cloture vote on the Mullin nomination, followed by a cloture vote on Colin McDonald's nomination for Assistant Attorney General. No House activity is scheduled, with the House set to reconvene Tuesday, March 24.
