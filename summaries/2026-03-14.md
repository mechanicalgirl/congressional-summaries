# Congressional Record Volume 172, No. 46 – Summary

## Senate Activities (March 12, 2026)

The Senate passed the Housing for the 21st Century Act (H.R. 6644) by a vote of 89-10, aimed at increasing housing supply in America. The chamber also approved a resolution celebrating the YMCA's 175th anniversary. Two cloture votes occurred: the first rejecting (51-46) a motion to close debate on the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act, and the second establishing a framework for voting on the Anna St. John federal judge nomination scheduled for Monday, March 16 at 5:30 p.m.

## Committee Actions

The Senate Armed Services Committee held hearings on U.S. European Command and Transportation Command posture for fiscal year 2027. The Commerce Committee favorably reported several nominations, including Matthew Anderson for NASA Deputy Administrator and others for transportation and commerce positions. The Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions Committee examined foreign influence in higher education. The Judiciary Committee favorably reported nominations for two U.S. Attorneys.

## New Legislation Introduced

Senators introduced 29 bills and 4 resolutions, including Senator Reed's ACTION for National Service Act to expand AmeriCorps participation toward one million positions within a decade, and Senator Durbin's bill prohibiting Social Security Trust Fund investments in cryptocurrency. Senator Welch introduced legislation seeking accountability for civilian casualties in Gaza.

## House Activities (March 12, 2026)

The House convened briefly at 9:30 a.m. and adjourned at 9:38 a.m. after routine proceedings. Representative Wittman served as Speaker pro tempore. The House introduced 44 bills (H.R. 7890-7933) and 6 resolutions, with constitutional authority statements provided for each.

## Committee Reports Filed

The Judiciary Committee reported H.R. 7640 on immigration enforcement, while the Natural Resources Committee reported H.R. 2709 on giant sequoia health and H.R. 6380 establishing Chiricahua National Park as a national park unit.
