# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-04-17)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-66/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-66/daily-digest)

The Senate continued consideration of H.J. Res. 140 regarding federal land withdrawal with a vote scheduled for Thursday, April 16, while also preparing to resume consideration of a district judge nomination. Recently signed public laws included the Holocaust Expropriated Art Recovery Act and legislation extending the SBIR and STTR programs. The House debated and passed resolutions on tax policy and Clean Air Act amendments, with multiple committee hearings scheduled to address the fiscal year 2027 budget proposal, cabinet nominations, and various oversight matters spanning from FTC operations to federal land management.

The House passed H. Res. 1156 supporting the Working Families Tax Cuts enacted in July 2025, along with three Clean Air Act amendments. Republicans highlighted provisions including eliminating taxes on tips and overtime, expanding child tax credits to $2,200 per child, and providing deductions for seniors on Social Security benefits, noting that average refunds reached $3,400. Democrats opposed the measures, arguing they primarily benefit wealthy individuals and corporations while cutting trillions from healthcare programs and adding $4.2 trillion to the national debt. On environmental matters, Republicans advanced bills to streamline Clean Air Act compliance by exempting foreign emissions and addressing wildfire impacts, while Democrats warned these measures would weaken public health protections.

Senate debate centered on H.J. Res. 140, a Congressional Review Act resolution that would overturn a 2023 mineral withdrawal protecting the Boundary Waters from copper-nickel sulfide mining. Senators from Minnesota and Wisconsin led opposition, arguing the Boundary Waters represents an irreplaceable ecosystem with clean water supporting a $1 billion recreation economy, that using the Congressional Review Act to overturn a three-year-old action sets a dangerous precedent, and that the action violates Tribal treaty rights under the 1854 Treaty of La Pointe. Evidence presented included polling showing 70 percent of Minnesotans support protection and opposition from Tribal nations, outdoor recreation businesses, and conservation organizations.

The Senate introduced twenty new bills addressing diverse policy areas and reported favorably on several executive nominations, including U.S. representatives to Southeast Asia and the African Development Bank. Senator Duckworth introduced a War Powers Resolution to end unauthorized military operations in Iran that have resulted in thirteen American deaths and significant economic costs through elevated gas and fertilizer prices
