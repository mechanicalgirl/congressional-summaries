# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-07-02)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-109/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-109/daily-digest)

On June 30, 2026, the House of Representatives introduced 35 public bills and 14 resolutions while conducting routine business, though it failed to approve a procedural rule for considering four major bills related to defense authorization, national security appropriations, disability insurance, and tax cuts, with the vote falling short 198 to 224. The House did unanimously pass Resolution 1399 with 420 votes directing the Committee on Ethics to preserve and release records on monetary settlements involving sexual harassment claims, addressing congressional accountability transparency. Meanwhile, the Senate remained out of session and was scheduled to reconvene on July 2, while multiple House committees held hearings and markups on topics ranging from youth sports commercialization to healthcare costs and tax policy.

The House proceedings included recognition of fallen firefighters and community contributors, with members honoring three wildland firefighters—Emily Barker, Nick Hutcherson, and Sydney Watson—who died fighting the Knowles fire in Colorado. A concurrent resolution directing removal of U.S. armed forces from hostilities in Lebanon failed to pass, receiving 189 votes in favor to 235 opposed, highlighting divisions within the chamber on military engagement decisions.

House Resolution 1398's defeat exposed partisan disagreements over defense and social spending priorities. Republicans supported the rule as necessary to advance a $1.15 trillion defense authorization that would rebuild military capacity, strengthen the defense industrial base, and increase military pay by 5 to 7 percent. Democrats opposed the measure, criticizing the massive defense spending, arguing it accompanied cuts to healthcare and food assistance, and objecting particularly to the attachment of the SAVE America Act voter identification bill to the defense measure through engrossment, which they characterized as a procedural shell game advancing what they called a voter suppression agenda lacking factual basis.
