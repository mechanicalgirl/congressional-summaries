# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-06-24)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-104/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-104/daily-digest)

On Monday, June 22, 2026, Congress conducted routine legislative business with the Senate passing the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act by an 85-5 vote, along with commemorative resolutions honoring victims of social media harms and an antisemitic attack in Boulder, Colorado. Cloture votes were entered on three judicial nominations for district court positions in Texas and Ohio, with final votes scheduled for subsequent days. The House introduced seven new bills addressing artificial intelligence workplace usage and farm bill assistance while receiving various executive communications from federal agencies regarding regulatory matters.

The Senate advanced several foreign policy and national security measures through amendments to the fiscal year 2027 defense authorization bill. These amendments address Iranian and Hezbollah influence operations in Latin America through sanctions and restrictions on cultural centers and media platforms, while establishing comprehensive protections for Taiwan's critical undersea infrastructure through monitoring systems and sanctions against vessels responsible for sabotage. Additional provisions express congressional concern about Uyghur detention programs in Xinjiang and direct the State Department to prioritize Uyghur rights while requiring language training for Foreign Service officers, and require Treasury assessments of Hong Kong's role in money laundering and sanctions violations.

Senate Amendment 5890, the Fix Our Forests Act, proposes comprehensive legislation to address wildfire risk through landscape-scale restoration and community protection strategies. The amendment establishes a Wildland Fire Intelligence Center to provide real-time scientific services for wildfire prevention and response, creates a Fireshed Registry to track wildfire exposure and hazardous fuels management, and expands collaborative tools allowing Indian tribes, counties, and special districts to participate in forest management projects. The legislation promotes prescribed fire use through liability protections and policy reforms while establishing community wildfire risk reduction programs focused on fire-resistant construction and defensible space improvements.

The Fix Our Forests Act includes provisions establishing a Wildland Fire Management Casualty Assistance Program to support families of firefighters killed or critically injured in the line of duty, with services including notification procedures, travel reimbursement, and case management. The legislation also enhances disaster assistance programs by establishing an Emergency Forest Watershed Program for National Forest System lands, expanding the Emergency Conservation Program to cover various emergency measures beyond fencing, and creating a unified disaster assistance intake process administered by the Federal Emergency Management Agency.
