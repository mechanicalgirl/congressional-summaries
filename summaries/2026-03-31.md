# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-03-31)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-58/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-58/daily-digest)

The House passed the Defending American Property Abroad Act of 2026 on March 27 with a 247-164 vote, granting the President authority to restrict vessels from entering U.S. ports if they operate through ports or harbors expropriated from American owners in Western Hemisphere countries with free trade agreements. The bill directly addresses Mexico's 2022 seizure of a port facility owned by Alabama-based Vulcan Materials Company following decades of lawful American operation. Supporters argued the measure was necessary after four years of failed diplomatic efforts to protect American investment and property rights, while opponents maintained it improperly weaponizes Coast Guard resources for a commercial dispute better resolved through existing trade arbitration and diplomatic channels.

The House also debated and approved H. Resolution 1142, allowing it to modify a Senate amendment to H.R. 7147 and extend Department of Homeland Security funding through a continuing resolution, though the vote revealed deep partisan divisions over border security and immigration enforcement funding. Republicans contended the Senate bill dangerously underfunded critical DHS components like Border Patrol and ICE operations, while Democrats characterized the Senate measure as genuine bipartisan compromise that properly funded essential agencies like TSA, FEMA, and the Coast Guard without giving unlimited resources to ICE, which they argued lacked adequate oversight. The House voted 213-203 to adopt the resolution, moving forward with a Republican-backed continuing resolution through May 22, 2026, rather than accepting the Senate's compromise measure.
