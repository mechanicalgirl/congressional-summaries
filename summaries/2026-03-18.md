# Congressional Digest Summary: March 16-17, 2026

## Senate Activity

The Senate convened on March 16, 2026, passing two resolutions honoring Reverend Jesse Louis Jackson Sr. and Coach Lou Holtz. Eight bills and two resolutions were introduced during the session. The primary Senate focus centered on the nomination of Anna St. John to be U.S. District Judge for Louisiana's Eastern District, with a cloture vote scheduled for 11:30 a.m. Tuesday, March 17, followed by a confirmation vote at 2:15 p.m. if cloture is invoked.

## House Floor Activity

The House passed twelve bills under suspension of the rules covering diverse topics including Holocaust art recovery, Iran sanctions, giant sequoia protection, national parks, emergency medical services, disaster reforestation, and the Lower Colorado River Multi-Species Conservation Program. Notable legislation includes the Chiricahua National Park Act, redesignating an Arizona monument as the nation's 64th national park, and the Post-Disaster Reforestation and Restoration Act to address wildfire recovery across Interior Department lands.

## Committee Hearings Scheduled

Senate committees plan extensive hearings for the week, including Armed Services examining Northern and Southern Command posture, Commerce addressing Section 230 platform regulations, Energy and Natural Resources focusing on water and power issues, and Judiciary considering bills on xylazine prohibition and numerous Department of Justice nominations.

## House Committee Actions

House committees will hold hearings on diverse subjects including health care costs, communications technology security, export-import bank oversight, and Department of Veterans Affairs programs. The House Rules Committee approved three bills for floor consideration: the Protecting Access for Hunters and Anglers Act, Deporting Fraudsters Act, and the Federal Working Animal Protection Act.

## New Legislation Introduced

Nineteen public bills and two resolutions were introduced Monday, including measures addressing breastfeeding jury duty exemptions, nitrous oxide sales restrictions, biosecurity workforce stabilization, and various veterans affairs provisions.

## Recognitions and Special Orders

The Extensions of Remarks section honored numerous individuals and organizations, including veterans, community leaders, educators, and organizations supporting veterans, disaster relief, and civil rights. Recognition also went to schools participating in a "Valentines for Veterans" program
