# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-05-22)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-86/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-86/daily-digest)

Congress reconvened on May 20, 2026, with both chambers advancing legislation on financial services, veterans' benefits, and cultural commemorations. The Senate passed several bills including TREY's Law prohibiting enforcement of non-disclosure agreements related to sexual abuse, the Stop Stealing Our Chips Act establishing whistleblower protections for semiconductor export control violations, and the Safe Cloud Storage Act limiting liability for cloud service providers storing child sexual abuse material for law enforcement. The Senate also confirmed judicial nominee Evan Rikhye to the District Court of the Virgin Islands by a 52-47 vote. Meanwhile, the House advanced multiple suspension bills with bipartisan support for deposit insurance modifications and Veterans Affairs facilities, while also passing H.R. 2616 by a 217-198 vote requiring parental consent before schools change a minor's gender markers or pronouns.

The House passed H.R. 2616, the Parental Rights Over the Education and Care of Their Kids Act, which requires public elementary and middle schools receiving federal funds to obtain parental consent before changing a student's gender markers, pronouns, or preferred name on school forms and sex-based accommodations. The bill also prohibits using federal funds to teach concepts related to gender ideology as defined in an executive order. Supporters argued the legislation restores parental rights and protects children from what they characterized as radical gender ideology, while opponents contended it constitutes federal overreach that could force teachers to out vulnerable transgender students to potentially unsafe home environments and effectively bans classroom discussion of transgender identities.

The House also considered three additional bills affecting veterans and cultural institutions. H.R. 1041 would prohibit the Department of Veterans Affairs from reporting veterans assigned a fiduciary to the national criminal background check system, protecting their Second Amendment rights. H.R. 6047 increases disability compensation for catastrophically injured veterans by approximately $10,000 annually and expands survivor benefits, though it finances these increases through higher refinance fees on veterans' home loans. H.R. 1329 removes restrictions on the Smithsonian American Women's History Museum's location on the National Mall and designates the South Monument site as preferred. Republicans supported these measures as addressing veterans' constitutional rights and honoring American contributions, while Democrats opposed financing veteran benefits through higher housing costs and criticized modifications to the women's history museum bill
