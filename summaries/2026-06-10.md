# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-06-10)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-96/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-96/daily-digest)

Both chambers of Congress convened on June 8, 2026, to advance legislative priorities focused on fraud prevention and government accountability. The Senate scheduled confirmation votes on judicial nominees, including Anthony W. Mattivi for United States District Judge for Kansas, while the House passed multiple fraud prevention bills under suspension of the rules, including the Pre-Payment Fraud Prevention and Treasury Data Access Act and the Federal Program Integrity and Fraud Prevention Act. These measures were designed to combat fraud and improper payments across federal agencies and programs through enhanced verification processes and sanctions against convicted fraudsters.

The House considered several significant foreign policy measures addressing national security and international partnerships. H.R. 7037, the DOMINANCE Act, establishes mechanisms to reduce U.S. and allied dependence on China for critical minerals and energy resources by creating a Bureau of Energy Security and Diplomacy within the State Department and launching educational programs to develop domestic expertise. H.R. 2505, the Block the Use of Transatlantic Technology in Iranian Made Drones Act, directs the Departments of Commerce, State, and Defense to develop coordinated strategies preventing Iran from acquiring U.S. and allied technologies used in unmanned aircraft systems that are being supplied to Russia, terrorist organizations, and other malign actors.

The House advanced two key fraud prevention bills designed to strengthen federal financial integrity. The Federal Program Integrity and Fraud Prevention Act creates an automatic three-year ban on federal contracts and grants for individuals convicted of specific federal fraud felonies, addressing a significant enforcement gap where over 95 percent of pandemic-related fraud convicts were not suspended from government business. The Pre-Payment Fraud Prevention and Treasury Data Access Act establishes governmentwide requirements for agencies to conduct anti-fraud risk evaluations and verify payments before issuance, substantially expanding use of the Treasury Department's underutilized Do Not Pay system to include additional datasets and verification procedures.

Senate Democrats formally challenged President Trump's Emergency Presidential Determination increasing refugee admissions from 7,500 to 17,500 for Fiscal Year 2026, arguing the administration failed to conduct appropriate consultations and lacked genuine grounds for the emergency declaration.
