# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-04-01)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-60/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-60/daily-digest)

Congress convened on March 30, 2026, with abbreviated sessions in both the Senate at 10:35 a.m. and the House at noon, with additional pro forma sessions scheduled for April 2, 2026. The Congressional Record for this date featured numerous Extensions of Remarks honoring community leaders, volunteers, and public servants, with special recognition given to Women's History Month honorees from Central Florida and recipients of 2026 Heroes Among Us awards who made exceptional contributions in healthcare, education, environmental restoration, and social services.

The legislative agenda included introduction of ten new bills and three resolutions covering diverse policy areas such as paid leave for reproductive health conditions, women's health research, premenstrual dysphoric disorder treatment access, diplomatic service reforms, and Medicare payment stability. Additional legislation addressed medical child support requirements, food assistance provisions, tax-exempt status regulations concerning foreign adversary-funded organizations, a diplomatic reserve corps establishment, terrorism threat assessments, and export control procedures. The House also received executive communications from multiple federal agencies, including reports from the Medicaid and CHIP Payment and Access Commission, State Department notifications regarding arms sales and Iranian strategy assessments, and various No FEAR Act compliance reports.
