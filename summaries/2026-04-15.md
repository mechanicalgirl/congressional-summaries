# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-04-15)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-64/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-64/daily-digest)

The Senate continued consideration of judicial nominations on April 13, 2026, advancing toward confirmation votes scheduled for the following day by a 50-44 party-line vote invoking cloture on John Thomas Shepherd's nomination to serve as United States District Judge for the Western District of Arkansas, with a confirmation vote set for 11:30 a.m. on Tuesday. A unanimous consent agreement was also reached establishing the timeline for a subsequent vote on Christopher R. Wolfe's nomination for the Western District of Texas at 2:15 p.m., contingent on cloture being invoked. The Senate Appropriations Committee announced subcommittee assignments for the 119th Congress with specific memberships reflecting recent changes including new appointments of Senators Fischer and Husted, while the chamber received multiple executive nominations for civilian positions at Treasury, the State Department, and the Department of Veterans Affairs, along with military promotions across various service branches.

During the session, senators engaged in extensive debate on major policy matters, particularly regarding the Trump administration's military operations against Iran. Republican senators, led by Senator Cornyn, defended Operation Epic Fury as necessary to neutralize Iran's threat and argued that the military campaign successfully degraded Iranian capabilities and forced the regime to the negotiating table. Democratic senators including Senator Schumer criticized the operation as poorly planned and economically damaging, contending that the administration proceeded without congressional authorization, adequate strategy, or consideration of consequences including surging energy costs and inflation.

Significant disagreements also emerged over immigration policy, with Senate Majority Leader Thune asserting that Democrats sought to undermine the Department of Homeland Security by refusing to fund ICE operations without additional oversight reforms. Democrats like Senator Durbin countered that reform measures including restrictions on warrantless home raids and unmasked enforcement operations were necessary to protect constitutional rights and ensure deportations targeted genuinely dangerous individuals rather than law-abiding residents. Senator Schumer further criticized the administration's handling of the Department of Justice regarding the purge of career prosecutors and hiring of January 6 insurrectionists, while Senator Grassley addressed inflation concerns and touted Republican efforts to provide tax relief for working families.
