# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-05-24)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-88/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-88/daily-digest)

On May 22, 2026, both chambers of Congress held brief pro forma sessions before adjourning for the Memorial Day weekend, with the Senate meeting for approximately 30 seconds and the House for just three minutes while conducting no votes or substantive business. Both chambers are scheduled to reconvene on Tuesday, May 26, 2026, with the Senate meeting at 8 a.m. and the House at 11 a.m., both in pro forma session.

During this legislative period, the House introduced thirteen bills and five resolutions addressing a diverse range of policy areas. Notable legislation included Representative Ed Case's Fireworks Trafficking and Money Laundering Prevention Act targeting illegal fireworks distribution, along with bills concerning affordable housing, veterans' suicide prevention, heat wave response, and privacy protections for electronic communications. Additionally, two appropriations bills were reported by committees: H.R. 9010 for Legislative Branch funding and H.R. 9022 for energy and water development through fiscal year 2027. House members also filed discharge petitions and added sponsors to existing legislation, while the Senate had no committee meetings scheduled for the week.
