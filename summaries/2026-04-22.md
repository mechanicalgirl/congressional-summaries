# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-04-22)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-69/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-69/daily-digest)

On Monday, April 20, 2026, the Senate confirmed District Judge Andrew B. Davis of Texas in a narrow 47-46 vote and adopted a memorial resolution honoring Chuck Norris, while the House added a new seat with the swearing in of Representative Analilia Mejia from New Jersey's 11th Congressional District. Both chambers advanced multiple bills and resolutions focused on emergency communications infrastructure, broadband deployment, and defense matters, with the House passing the First Responder Network Authority Reauthorization Act on a bipartisan basis to extend FirstNet authority through 2037 with enhanced oversight requirements. Senators Bennet and Hickenlooper submitted a resolution commemorating the 10th anniversary of the Columbine Day of Service, recognizing how the Columbine community transformed the tragedy of the 1999 shooting into a global movement that drew over 1,600 participants from multiple states and countries in 2024. The Senate also saw introduction of the America Grows Act of 2026 by Senators Durbin and Moran, which seeks to significantly increase federal funding for agricultural research agencies beginning in fiscal 2027 through 2037, with appropriations protected from sequestration and budgetary scorecards.
