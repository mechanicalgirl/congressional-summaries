# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-07-01)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-108/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-108/daily-digest)

The Senate held a brief pro forma session on June 29, 2026, with no substantive business, while the House remained highly active with multiple legislative initiatives. The House passed numerous bills under suspension of rules addressing pandemic unemployment fraud recovery, healthcare efficiency, terrorism risk insurance reauthorization, postal facility designations, child online safety, grid security, rural utility cybersecurity, and energy sector support. The chamber also began consideration of a resolution directing the President to remove Armed Forces from hostilities in Lebanon under the War Powers Resolution, though further proceedings were postponed. The Rules Committee granted a structured rule for the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2027 consideration, setting parameters for amendments and debate.

Representative McClellan of Virginia delivered a speech on America's 250th anniversary, emphasizing that while the nation was founded on principles of liberty and justice, these ideals remain unfulfilled for many Americans, particularly Black and marginalized communities. She traced historical struggles from slavery through the civil rights movement to present-day challenges including voter suppression efforts, and introduced her "All American Family Agenda" to address practical barriers families face in affording housing, healthcare, childcare, and maternal care. McClellan stressed that achieving the nation's founding promise requires protecting voting rights, investing in family support services, and ensuring equitable access to life's necessities for all Americans.

The House advanced several bipartisan energy security measures, including bills to establish an Energy Threat Analysis Center, reauthorize cybersecurity grant programs for rural utilities, elevate DOE's emergency response functions, and require states to incorporate local distribution system security into energy plans. These bills address growing concerns about cyberattacks and physical threats to America's aging energy infrastructure, particularly affecting rural and smaller utilities lacking resources for sophisticated defense. The House also passed the Kids Internet and Digital Safety Act with bipartisan support, which establishes safeguards for social media platforms, restricts harmful design features, mandates parental controls, protects children's privacy, prohibits targeted advertising to minors, and requires age verification for pornography websites.

The House completed action on the Terrorism Risk Insurance Program Reauthorization Act, extending federal terrorism risk insurance coverage through 2034 and increasing transparency in the certification process. The bill increases the loss threshold triggering program review from five million to ten million dollars and establishes clearer timelines and public notice
