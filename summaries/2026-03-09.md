# Summary of Congressional Record Vol. 172, No. 42 (March 5, 2026)

## Senate Activity

The Senate continued consideration of the Housing for the 21st Century Act (H.R. 6644), with multiple amendments pending on a substitute proposal. Senators debated and proposed various amendments related to workforce housing, housing for elderly or disabled persons, and proximity to environmental hazard sites. Cloture motions were filed on both an amendment and the underlying bill, with votes scheduled for March 9, 2026. The Senate passed the Children and Teens' Online Privacy Protection Act (S. 836) and the Servicemembers' Credit Monitoring Enhancement Act (S. 2074). A cloture vote on the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act failed, with the Senate rejecting the motion to proceed. Lt. Gen. Joshua M. Rudd's nomination to become General was introduced, with a cloture vote scheduled for March 9 at 5:30 p.m.

## House Activity

The House passed the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act for fiscal year 2026 (H.R. 7744) by a vote of 221-209. The chamber also passed H. Res. 1099, reaffirming that Iran remains the largest state sponsor of terrorism, by a vote of 372-53-2. However, H. Con. Res. 38, directing the President to remove armed forces from unauthorized hostilities in Iran, failed to pass with a vote of 212-219. The House introduced 49 public bills and 4 resolutions, bringing various legislative proposals before committees for consideration.

## Committee Hearings and Markups

Multiple House committees held substantive meetings. The Agriculture Committee advanced the Farm, Food, and National Security Act. The Appropriations Committee held hearings on Inspectors General for Transportation and Housing departments, and Member Day hearings on appropriations matters. The Armed Services Committee heard testimony on U.S. defense strategy and posture. The Education and Workforce Committee marked up eight bills addressing child care fraud and accountability. The Energy and Commerce Committee considered nine bills covering internet safety, energy security, and cybersecurity issues. The Judiciary Committee advanced legislation on sanctuary policies and ATM crimes.
