# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-05-01)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-75/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-75/daily-digest)

On April 29, 2026, the Senate passed several bills addressing whistleblower protections, small business fraud enforcement, surety bond programs, and pipeline safety, while also confirming Robert Cekada as Director of the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives. Concurrently, the House debated the Farm, Food, and National Security Act of 2026, a comprehensive farm bill designed to extend agricultural programs through fiscal year 2031, which Chairman Thompson presented as thoroughly bipartisan with input from over 500 agriculture stakeholder groups. Democrats opposed the legislation, arguing it failed to address farm bankruptcies, provide emergency assistance for rising input costs, and restore food assistance cuts, while also raising concerns about pesticide liability shields and conservation program reductions.

The farm bill includes extensive provisions for forest conservation programs, rural development initiatives, and forest management reforms. New forest conservation easement programs allow the acquisition of easements on eligible forestland with compensation for landowners, while amendments to rural development programs establish innovation centers, expand support for meat processing facilities and childcare services, and strengthen infrastructure for water and wastewater systems. Forest management provisions expand agroforestry initiatives, establish a White Oak Restoration Fund, streamline hazard tree removal procedures, and create categorical exclusions for electric utility vegetation management without environmental assessments.

The legislation also implements comprehensive federal crop insurance reforms, including expanded coverage for revenue losses from commodity price declines, enhanced support for beginning and veteran farmers, and new pilot programs for specialty crops such as wine grapes affected by wildfire smoke and mushroom production. Debate amendments addressed diverse agricultural issues including biostimulants, composting practices, dairy innovation, tree-planting grants, and an expanded Office of Seafood within USDA to support commercial fishermen, while controversial amendments regarding pesticide labeling and SNAP-eligible foods generated substantial debate over federal authority and program effectiveness.

In other legislative action, the House passed the Foreign Intelligence Accountability Act, reauthorizing the FISA section 702 surveillance program for three years with additional safeguards including monthly FBI query reviews, mandatory attorney approval for searches involving U.S. persons, and independent audits. The Senate also passed the Pipeline Safety Act, representing a comprehensive overhaul of federal pipeline regulations with increased funding through 2030 and enhanced transparency requirements.
