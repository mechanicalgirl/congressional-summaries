# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-06-13)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-99/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-99/daily-digest)

On Thursday, June 11, 2026, Congress conducted routine legislative business with the Senate passing four bills focused on environmental conservation, historical recognition, and social support services. The Senate advanced the Cross-Boundary Wildfire Solutions Act, the Theodore Roosevelt National Park Wild Horses Protection Act, the First Rhode Island Regiment Congressional Gold Medal Act, and the Recruiting Families Using Data Act. Additionally, the Senate voted to close debate on Justin D. Smith's nomination to the Eighth Circuit Court of Appeals with confirmation scheduled for Monday, and confirmed Brock Dahl as Legal Adviser of the Department of State by a narrow 49-44 margin.

The House held a brief pro forma session and introduced fifty-three public bills and twelve resolutions, with significant activity including consideration of an appropriations bill for fiscal year 2027 covering Labor, Health and Human Services, and Education. Representative Al Green delivered remarks on discriminatory rhetoric, citing academic research on racist language disproportionately directed at Black Americans and women, while other members recognized local achievements and military sacrifice.

The House debated H. Res. 1335, condemning fraud in federal programs and supporting government-wide fraud prevention reforms. Republicans emphasized investigations into Minnesota's social services fraud schemes, while Democrats pointed to comparable fraud cases in Republican-led states and concerns about presidential pardons for convicted fraudsters. The House voted on legislation extending Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act authorities, which failed to achieve the required two-thirds majority, and approved a concurrent resolution authorizing use of Emancipation Hall for a Semiquincentennial Congressional Time Capsule ceremony scheduled for June 24, 2026.

Senators Barrasso and Lummis introduced S. 4765, the "Let America Build Act of 2026," a comprehensive energy and permitting reform bill addressing oil and gas leasing, mineral development, and natural gas infrastructure. The legislation establishes strict timelines for permit decisions, limits litigation challenges to sixty days, and implements a cooperative federalism framework delegating federal oil and gas permitting to states. The bill also expands liquefied natural gas export authority with expedited approval processes and reforms mineral leasing by establishing environmental review timelines of one to two years for major projects.
