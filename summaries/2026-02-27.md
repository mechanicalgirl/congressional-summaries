# Digest of Congressional Events - February 25, 2026

## Senate Actions and Nominations

The Senate convened on February 25, 2026, and confirmed John DeLeeuw of Texas to be a Member of the National Transportation Safety Board for the remainder of the term expiring December 31, 2026, by a vote of 50-45. The Senate also reached a unanimous consent agreement regarding the nomination of Ryan McCormack of Virginia to be Under Secretary of Transportation for Policy, scheduling a cloture vote for 11:45 a.m. on February 26 and a confirmation vote at 1:45 p.m. if cloture is invoked.

## Committee Hearings and Business

Multiple Senate committees held hearings and business meetings on February 25. The Committee on Energy and Natural Resources concluded hearings on nominations for the Bureau of Land Management Director, Under Secretary of Energy, and Federal Energy Regulatory Commission positions. The Committee on Environment and Public Works examined the Water Resources Development Act focusing on Army Corps of Engineers projects. The Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions Committee held hearings on the nomination of Casey Means to be Surgeon General.

## Legislation and Bills Introduced

The House introduced 51 public bills and 9 resolutions. The Senate introduced 18 bills and three resolutions. Notable bills include the Delilah Law (S. 3917), prohibiting commercial driver's licenses for non-citizens, and the We Can't Wait Act (S. 3924), allowing disabled individuals to access Social Security Disability Insurance benefits during the five-month waiting period. The Julius Rosenwald and Rosenwald Schools National Historical Park Act (S. 3925) was introduced to commemorate the philanthropist and historical schools.

## House Floor Activity

The House passed H.R. 4758, the Homeowner Energy Freedom Act, by a vote of 210-199, repealing provisions of the Inflation Reduction Act relating to taxpayer subsidies for home electrification. The House also passed three bills from the Committee on Financial Services relating to energy conservation standards, scientific evidence in federal rules, and securities regulations.
