# Summary of Congressional Record, Vol. 172, No. 44 (March 10, 2026)

**Senate Business and Legislation**
On March 10, 2026, the Senate continued consideration of H.R. 6644, the Housing for the 21st Century Act. The chamber voted 89-9 to invoke cloture on the Scott/Warren Amendment No. 4308, halting further debate on this substitute amendment. Senator Kennedy called a quorum call, which was subsequently rescinded.

**Passed Legislation**
The Senate passed S. 327, the HONOR Act (Hindering Oppressive Nations from Obtaining Revenue Act), by unanimous consent. This bill denies foreign tax credits and deductions for taxes paid to the Russian Federation, targeting entities providing financial support to Russia amid its involvement with Iran.

**Judicial Confirmation**
Lieutenant General Joshua M. Rudd was confirmed to the rank of General in the United States Army by a vote of 71-29. Senator Van Hollen opposed the nomination over concerns about Rudd's lack of cyber and signals intelligence expertise for his anticipated position leading U.S. Cyber Command and the NSA.

**Bills and Resolutions Introduced**
Senators introduced 13 bills (S. 4033-4045) and 10 resolutions, including measures addressing rare earth depletion, Alzheimer's research funding, and multiple resolutions requesting information on human rights practices in Uzbekistan, Ghana, Honduras, Equatorial Guinea, Kosovo, Guatemala, and Cameroon—particularly regarding treatment of non-citizens removed by the U.S. government.

**Concurrent Resolution on Abortion Providers**
Senators Hirono and 12 colleagues introduced S. Con. Res. 28, designating March 10, 2026 as "Abortion Provider Appreciation Day" to recognize abortion providers and staff for ensuring patient access to care.
