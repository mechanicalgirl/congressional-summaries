# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-07-15)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-113/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-113/daily-digest)

Congress adjourned on July 13, 2026, to observe Senator Lindsey Graham's passing and resumed business on July 14, with the Senate continuing consideration of Matthew A. Schwartz's nomination for United States Circuit Judge for the Second Circuit, the swearing-in of Senator-designate Darline Graham, and a cloture vote on S. 4784, the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2027. The House took up four bills under consideration rules addressing daylight saving time, appropriations, veterans' care, and privacy in purchases. Through the first half of 2026, Congress has enacted 32 public bills with the Senate holding 100 days in session and the House 90 days, while confirming 5,196 nominees from approximately 10,488 total nominations submitted.

The Congressional Record from July 13, 2026, reflects standard legislative activity mixed with tributes to departing members, including recognition of Agatucci's Restaurant in Illinois approaching its centennial and Jamie Robinette's retirement after 25 years in congressional offices. The House passed several post office naming bills designating facilities in Ohio and Virginia for former President Ulysses S. Grant and former Congressman Gerald E. Connolly, with extensive remarks praising Connolly's legacy in public service and civil rights advocacy. Representative Garbarino's committee advanced six bills addressing aviation security, airport operations, and fentanyl detection efforts, including pilot programs for expedited TSA screening and requirements for the Department of Homeland Security to develop performance metrics for combating illicit fentanyl trafficking.

Representatives also honored numerous constituents and community institutions during this legislative session. Representative Joyce of Pennsylvania delivered tributes to Don Detwiler, a Blair County business leader who recently passed away and served as president and CEO of New Enterprise Stone & Lime, and recognized the Williamsburg Volunteer Fire Department's century of service since its 1926 founding. Joyce congratulated two high school baseball teams on championship victories, including Bermudian Springs High School winning the PIAA 3A State Championship and Greencastle-Antrim High School capturing the PIAA 5A State Championship in a historic 14-inning contest. Joyce also honored the Smithfield Volunteer Fire Company of Huntingdon
