# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-04-19)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-68/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-68/daily-digest)

On Friday, April 17, 2026, the Senate passed H.R. 8322, extending Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act authorities through April 30, 2026, while Senator Wyden raised concerns about FISA abuses and advocated for reforms balancing security and civil liberties. The Senate also introduced S. 4343 regarding congressional retirement benefits for members convicted of certain felonies and S. 4344 proposing a three-year extension of FISA Section 702 authorities. Additional House-passed measures were processed, including bills on Haiti's temporary protected status and Clean Air Act amendments. Senator Wyden lifted his hold on Colonel Thomas M. Siverts's promotion to brigadier general after reviewing relevant materials and receiving assurances about the nominee's opposition to anti-Semitism and racism.

The Senate is scheduled to reconvene at 3 p.m. on Monday, April 20, 2026, to vote on Andrew B. Davis's nomination as United States District Judge for the Western District of Texas. The House will convene at noon the same day with its program to be announced later. Multiple committee meetings are scheduled for Monday, including Senate Armed Services Subcommittee hearings on nuclear weapons programs and House committee hearings on broadband deployment, infrastructure, and energy legislation, along with a closed hearing on fiscal year 2027 compartmented programs budget requests.
