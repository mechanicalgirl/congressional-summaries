# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-05-15)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-81/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-81/daily-digest)

On May 13, 2026, both chambers of Congress conducted significant legislative activity focused on budget authorization, defense matters, and law enforcement issues. The Senate rejected motions regarding the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection, confirmed Kevin Warsh as Federal Reserve Chairman, and scheduled votes on government shutdown pay withholding and pending nominations. The House passed the Nationwide Consumer and Fuel Retailer Choice Act addressing ethanol waivers, agreed to resolutions supporting law enforcement and condemning antisemitism, and began consideration of a War Powers Resolution directing removal of armed forces from hostilities against Iran.

Multiple House and Senate committees held hearings and markups addressing fiscal year 2027 budget requests for federal agencies including the Defense Department, Interior Department, and Environmental Protection Agency. Committee discussions covered cybersecurity readiness, nuclear energy policy, social media's impact on children's safety, tick-borne diseases, stem cell research, and dementia treatment access. House members also offered tributes recognizing first responders, educators, community leaders, and fallen public servants throughout the country.

The House debated and passed H. Con. Res. 96 supporting law enforcement officers with a 243-173 vote, though partisan divisions emerged over crime statistics and police support policies. Republicans cited declining homicide rates and violent crime, attributing improvements to the Trump administration's law-and-order focus, while Democrats argued crime decreases began under the Biden administration and criticized Republicans for cutting public safety grants and diverting federal agents from fighting organized crime. The House also considered H.R. 1346, the Nationwide Consumer and Fuel Retailer Choice Act, which would permit year-round sale of E15 fuel blends to lower gasoline prices and increase domestic corn demand. Opposition centered on impacts to small refineries facing estimated compliance cost increases of $100 million annually and the bill's $2.27 billion deficit impact without offsets.

The Senate voted 49-50 to reject a motion discharging Senator Merkley's resolution directing removal of U.S. Armed Forces from hostilities with Iran. Democrats argued that military operations including naval blockades constitute ongoing acts of war requiring Congressional approval under the War Powers Act, while Republicans countered that major fighting has halted and a ceasefire has held for over a month.
