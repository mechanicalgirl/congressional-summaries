# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-07-20)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-117/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-117/daily-digest)

Congress convened briefly on Saturday, July 18, 2026, with the House meeting for only two minutes to conduct routine matters including the pledge of allegiance, prayer, approval of the journal, and adjournment. Representative Michael Simpson served as Speaker pro tempore for the day, while the Senate remained adjourned until Monday, July 20 at 3 p.m. The House reconvened the same day at noon for morning-hour debate and 2 p.m. for legislative business.

Several significant bills were introduced during this brief session, including H.R. 9770 providing continuing appropriations for fiscal year 2027, H.R. 9771 and H.R. 9772 addressing foreign contributions to political committees and tax-exempt organizations respectively, and H.J. Res. 202 providing congressional disapproval of an EPA rule regarding California's vehicle emission standards. Additionally, two committee reports were filed addressing Medicare payment rules for manual wheelchairs and establishing the congressional budget for fiscal years 2027-2036.

Multiple executive communications concerning regulatory rules from federal agencies, primarily the FAA and EPA regarding airworthiness directives and environmental standards, were referred to appropriate House committees for further consideration and action.
