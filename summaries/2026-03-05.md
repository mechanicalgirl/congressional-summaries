# Congressional Record Summary: Volume 172, No. 40 (March 3, 2026)

## Senate and House Actions

The Senate convened to continue debate on H.R. 6644, the Housing for the 21st Century Act, with a motion to proceed post-cloture. The Senate scheduled a vote on the motion for 11 a.m. Wednesday, with a potential additional vote at 4 p.m. on Senator Kaine's motion to discharge S.J. Res. 104 regarding removal of U.S. forces from Iran hostilities. The House began consideration of H. Con. Res. 38, directing the President to withdraw armed forces from unauthorized hostilities in Iran.

## Major Legislation Passed

The Senate unanimously passed S. 3971, the Small Business Innovation and Economic Security Act, extending the SBIR and STTR programs through 2031. The bill implements foreign due diligence requirements, establishes strategic breakthrough awards, reduces administrative burden through proposal limits, and enhances Phase III commercialization support while improving transparency and data collection.

## Iran War Debate

Congress engaged in heated debate over President Trump's military operations in Iran initiated over the weekend. Democratic lawmakers, including Senators Warren, Schumer, and McBride, criticized the war as unconstitutional, launched without clear justification, and resulting in six American deaths. Republicans, led by Senator Barrasso, defended the operation as necessary counterterrorism, citing Iran's 47-year antagonism toward the United States and recent missile attacks on regional allies.

## DHS Funding Crisis

Democrats blocked Department of Homeland Security appropriations in the second shutdown of fiscal 2026, withholding over $20 million for ICE body cameras and deescalation training. Republicans argued Democrats prioritized politics over essential security functions, while Senator Tillis demanded performance data on deportation operations and threatened nomination holds until questions were answered.

## Veterans' Benefits Fight
