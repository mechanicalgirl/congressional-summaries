# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-04-29)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-73/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-73/daily-digest)

Congress convened on Monday, April 27, 2026, with the Senate passing a resolution congratulating the University of South Carolina Aiken women's polo team and advancing the nomination of Robert Cekada for Director of the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives through a cloture vote. The House introduced 40 public bills and 3 resolutions while passing 11 bills under suspension of the rules, including measures addressing IRS modernization, taxpayer protections, child support enforcement, and clergy tax exemptions. Both chambers adjourned with plans to reconvene for a joint meeting to receive His Majesty King Charles III of the United Kingdom.

Throughout the week beginning April 28, both chambers scheduled extensive committee hearings and markups focused on fiscal year 2027 budget requests across federal agencies. The Senate Armed Services Committee examined the posture of U.S. Special Operations Command and Cyber Command, while Senate Appropriations Subcommittees held budget hearings for NASA, the Department of Education, the National Nuclear Security Administration, and other agencies. The House conducted its joint meeting with the British monarch on Tuesday morning and various committees held hearings on topics ranging from AI and grid modernization to space security and veterans' benefits.

Executive departments submitted multiple rules and reports to Congress, including guidance on cryptocurrency assets from the Commodity Futures Trading Commission and leveraged lending standards from the Treasury Department. The House processed bills focused on tax administration and IRS modernization, including measures to require electronic scanning of paper tax returns and improve the whistleblower program. Congress also advanced legislation addressing customs enforcement of intellectual property rights and considered bipartisan bills affecting taxpayers and workers, such as provisions extending disaster tax relief to wildfire victims and allowing clergy members to revoke Social Security exemptions.

The Senate addressed significant budget and policy disputes, with particular focus on the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program, medical research funding cuts, and immigration policy impacts on healthcare workers. Senators expressed concerns about the administration's proposed elimination of LIHEAP and restrictions on visa processing for foreign medical professionals, arguing these policies harm vulnerable populations and America's medical research leadership. The chamber also honored the late Congressman David Scott and addressed political violence following weekend incidents.
