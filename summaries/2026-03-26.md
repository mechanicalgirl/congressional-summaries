# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-03-26)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-55/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-55/daily-digest)

On March 24, 2026, Congress advanced significant legislation across both chambers. The Senate moved forward on the SAVE America Act with multiple pending amendments regarding voter identification and election procedures, confirmed Colin McDonald as Assistant Attorney General, and passed the Emergency Conservation Program Improvement Act to help agricultural producers access emergency funds. The Senate also held hearings on munitions, cybersecurity, and arms control. Meanwhile, the House passed thirteen bills under suspension of rules covering topics from boxing safety to broadband access in Appalachia, and approved a rule governing consideration of four additional bills related to homeland defense, the District of Columbia, and property abroad.

Members of the Congressional Black Caucus held a Special Order hour to commemorate Women's History Month, focusing on the historical and ongoing struggles of Black women in America. Representative McClellan traced Black women's fight for inclusion from 1619 to the present, highlighting figures like Shirley Chisholm and Fannie Lou Hamer while condemning policies that have displaced approximately 300,000 Black women from the federal workforce. Representative McIver called for resistance to what she described as coordinated attacks on Black women's freedom through workforce displacement, healthcare cuts, and voting rights erosion. Representative McClellan detailed threats to programs like SNAP and Medicaid expansion and criticized the administration's efforts to dismantle diversity and inclusion initiatives and the Department of Education, arguing these actions prevent the nation from healing historical wounds.

The House passed H.R. 6480, directing the General Services Administration to submit annual reports on its real estate portfolio and reinstate transparency measures discontinued nearly a decade ago. The bill requires detailed accounting of leased and owned properties including square footage, costs, vacancies, and relocation plans, with supporters from both parties emphasizing the importance of congressional oversight, particularly given recent significant changes to the federal real estate footprint under the Trump administration's DOGE initiatives.

During a final session on March 27, 2026, the Senate confirmed Colin McDonald as Assistant Attorney General and Lieutenant Colonel Alan Armstrong as interim Senator from Oklahoma. Significant debate occurred over the SAVE America Act's voter identification amendments, with Democrats characterizing them as voter suppression while Republicans defended them as election security measures.
