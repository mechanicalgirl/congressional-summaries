# Congressional Digest Summary: March 5-9, 2026

## Senate Activities

The Senate convened on March 5, 2026, and introduced 27 bills and 9 resolutions while passing two measures: the Children and Teens' Online Privacy Protection Act and the Servicemembers' Credit Monitoring Enhancement Act. The chamber continued deliberation on the Housing for the 21st Century Act (H.R. 6644), with multiple amendments pending consideration.

A cloture motion was entered on the Scott/Warren Amendment No. 4308, with a vote scheduled for March 9 at 5:30 p.m. The Senate also began consideration of Lt. Gen. Joshua M. Rudd's nomination to be General in the United States Army, with cloture voting likewise scheduled for Monday.

The Senate rejected a motion to proceed to the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act by a vote of 51-45, failing to achieve the required three-fifths majority. A unanimous consent agreement established that filing deadlines for amendments to the housing bill would be March 9 at 3:30 p.m.

## House Activities

The House passed the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act (H.R. 7744) by a vote of 221-209 and rejected a motion to recommit by 212-217. The chamber also reaffirmed that Iran remains the largest state sponsor of terrorism through H. Res. 1099 by a vote of 372-53, while rejecting H. Con. Res. 38 directing removal of U.S. armed forces from Iran by 212-219.

The House introduced 49 public bills (H.R. 7811-7859) and 4 resolutions during the session. A district work period was designated from March 5-15, 2026, allowing members to return to their home districts.
