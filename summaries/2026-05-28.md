# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-05-28)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-89/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-89/daily-digest)

Congress held abbreviated sessions on May 26, 2026, with both chambers meeting in pro forma sessions. The Senate convened for 29 seconds while the House briefly adjourned, with both bodies scheduled to reconvene the following week. During this time, the House introduced 22 new bills and one resolution, and the Education and Workforce Committee reported two bills related to identity fraud verification in federal student financial aid programs.

The Extensions of Remarks section featured extensive survivor testimony from a May 12 House Oversight Committee hearing on Jeffrey Epstein's crimes. Survivors including Maria Farmer, Courtney Wild, and Annie Farmer documented decades of abuse and detailed institutional failures by law enforcement and the justice system. They highlighted the FBI's failure to act on initial reports, a secret non-prosecution agreement that violated victims' rights, and the Department of Justice's unauthorized release of survivors' personal information when documents were unsealed.

Survivors called for significant legislative reforms, including amendments to the Crime Victims' Rights Act to ensure meaningful victim consultation before prosecution agreements and establish consequences for violations. They also requested transparency requirements, attorney fee provisions, and urged Congress to prevent any pardon or commutation of Ghislaine Maxwell. Additionally, survivors called for congressional investigations into Epstein's enablers and co-conspirators, as well as financial investigations into the network that facilitated the abuse.
