# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-05-31)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-91/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-91/daily-digest)

Congress convened on Friday, May 29, 2026, with the House in brief session while the Senate remained adjourned. The House introduced 38 public bills and 7 resolutions addressing diverse policy matters including Head Start program effectiveness, COVID-19 vaccine-related military separations, water treaty mechanisms, and Medicare international coverage, along with various other legislative initiatives. Two committee reports were filed concerning temporary assistance for needy families and pandemic-era unemployment compensation recovery, while the House appointed Theogene Anthony Mills III of Louisiana to the Commission on International Religious Freedom and processed executive communications from federal agencies on matters such as pipeline safety and tariff extensions on Turkish archaeological artifacts.

The Extensions of Remarks section featured numerous tributes recognizing individuals and organizations for their contributions, including centenarian James A. Bussey's community service in Georgia, the 100th anniversaries of two American Legion Post fire departments in West Henrietta and Giles-Luce, and honors for military veterans during Military Appreciation Month. The Senate will reconvene on Monday, June 1, at 3 p.m. to consider a cloture motion on the nomination of Kathleen S. Lane for United States District Judge for Montana, while the House will return Tuesday, June 2, at noon.
