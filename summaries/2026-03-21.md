# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-03-21)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-50/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-50/daily-digest)

On March 19, 2026, the Senate continued consideration of amendments to S. 1383, the SAVE America Act, with multiple cloture motions pending and procedural agreements reached for continued proceedings. The Senate also passed S. 4123, prohibiting preferential airport screening for Members of Congress, and agreed to resolutions recognizing the U.S.-Japan Alliance and designating March 21 as National Women in Agriculture Day. The House passed H.R. 4638, the Bill to Outlaw Wounding of Official Working Animals Act, by a vote of 228-190, and introduced numerous bills addressing fentanyl-related substances, privacy rights, and financial regulations.

Representative Al Green of Texas delivered an extended address criticizing the cryptocurrency industry's alleged efforts to influence Congress through massive campaign spending and lobbying to prevent implementation of central bank digital currencies. Green contended that the crypto industry prioritizes campaign donations over constituent interests and seeks to derail legislation like the Senate's housing bill to achieve its policy goals. Representative Tom Grothman of Wisconsin addressed policy concerns including preservation of sheltered workshops for individuals with disabilities, reduction of federal employment, opposition to surgical interventions for transgender youth, and reform of tax policies that he argues penalize marriage and discourage family formation.

Amendment SA 4688, submitted by Senator Wyden with Senators Lujan and Van Hollen, proposes the "Keeping Obstetrics Local Act" addressing critical gaps in maternal health care access, particularly in rural and safety-net hospitals. The amendment establishes Medicaid payment rate requirements for maternity services beginning in fiscal year 2027, expands coverage to include 12-month postpartum Medicaid and CHIP coverage, invests in maternal health care workforce development, and mandates public disclosure of obstetrics data and unit closures with 180-day advance notice requirements.

Senator Durbin introduced the Forever Chemical Regulation and Accountability Act of 2026 (S. 4153), which establishes comprehensive regulations to phase out nonessential uses of perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances known as forever chemicals.
