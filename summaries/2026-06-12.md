# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-06-12)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-98/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-98/daily-digest)

Congress convened on June 10, 2026, with significant legislative activity across both chambers focused on government accountability and fraud prevention. The House passed three major bills addressing improper payments and financial management: the No Aid for Ghost Students Act, which requires identity fraud detection for student financial aid applications; the Fraud Prevention and Accountability Act, establishing new fraud prevention functions within the Treasury Department; and the Stopping Fraudulent Payments Act, authorizing agencies to pause and segment payments. Additionally, the House suspended rules to pass measures reforming payment integrity requirements and modifying the government-wide financial management plan. The Senate passed several bills related to law enforcement and criminal justice, including measures addressing prison contraband, post-traumatic stress disorder treatment for public safety officers, and forensic activities support.

The House passed two major fraud prevention bills with significant implications for federal spending oversight. H.R. 8312, the Fraud Prevention and Accountability Act, passed 240-181 and establishes a permanent Inspector General for Fraud, Accountability, and Recovery within the Treasury Department while creating a governmentwide data analytics program to combat fraud and improper payments. H.R. 8464, the Stopping Fraudulent Payments Act, passed 218-200 after a narrow defeat of a motion to recommit, authorizing the Treasury Department and federal agencies to pause, condition, or segment payments presenting elevated fraud risk before disbursement. Democrats expressed concerns that the bill's broad authority could be weaponized for political purposes, citing recent Medicaid and disaster relief suspensions, while Republicans argued the measures are essential safeguards against the hundreds of billions in annual federal fraud losses.

The Senate advanced several significant criminal justice and law enforcement bills during this session. The Carla Walker Act establishes grant programs totaling $10 million annually through fiscal year 2031 for forensic genetic genealogy DNA analysis at state and local law enforcement agencies with comprehensive safeguards including warrant requirements and informed consent provisions. The Senate also passed the Promoting Police Leadership Act, which tasks the Attorney General with developing training curricula for command-level law enforcement personnel covering leadership, critical incident response, and evidence-based policing strategies.
