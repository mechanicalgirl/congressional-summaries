# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-05-14)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-80/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-80/daily-digest)

The Senate confirmed Kevin Warsh as Chairman of the Federal Reserve Board by a 51-45 vote and scheduled procedural votes on several matters including a motion regarding U.S. Armed Forces in Iran, a resolution on Senate pay during government shutdowns, and Consumer Financial Protection Bureau reviews for May 13. The Senate also confirmed military nominations and received new federal judicial nominations for circuit and district court positions, while the House passed six suspension bills addressing financial regulation, organized retail crime, firearms background checks, and law enforcement matters. Two new public laws were signed addressing FCC vetting processes and FERC hydropower timelines.

The Congressional Black Caucus raised urgent concerns about voting rights, warning that the Supreme Court's recent decision in Louisiana v. Callais has enabled Republican-controlled state legislatures to rapidly redraw congressional maps to dismantle majority-Black districts in multiple Southern states including Florida, Tennessee, Louisiana, Alabama, and South Carolina. Members drew parallels between these current efforts and historical Jim Crow era disenfranchisement tactics, characterizing this moment as a critical juncture in American history requiring immediate action to restore voting rights protections that were previously gutted by the Shelby County v. Holder decision in 2013.

The House advanced several bipartisan financial services bills aimed at reducing regulatory burden on community banks and credit unions while maintaining consumer protections. These measures include allowing well-capitalized institutions with assets under $6 billion to alternate between full and limited examination scopes, raising asset thresholds for examination cycles from $3 billion to $6 billion, codifying mentor-protégé programs to support smaller and minority-focused institutions, and protecting domestic shrimp producers from subsidized foreign competition through restrictions on international financing for competing operations.

The Senate processed numerous military nominations including hundreds of Air Force officer promotions to lieutenant colonel and Army and Navy officer nominations, along with six judicial nominees for federal district and circuit courts. The Senate also approved arms sales notifications to Middle Eastern allies including Israel, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain, and the United Arab Emirates involving Patriot missile systems and precision-guided munitions, as well as sales to the Philippines for $150 million in helicopters and to Canada for $540 million in sustainment support, all justified on national security grounds or routine defense cooperation.
