# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-03-25)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-54/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-54/daily-digest)

On Monday, March 23, 2026, the Senate confirmed Markwayne Mullin of Oklahoma as Secretary of Homeland Security, and he immediately resigned from his Senate seat. The Senate also passed several measures including S. 921 directing the Secretary of Health and Human Services to study fentanyl testing procedures in hospital emergency departments, S. 4164 making technical corrections to the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2026, and S. Res. 660 honoring six Air Force servicemembers.

On Tuesday, March 24, the Senate resumed consideration of the House message regarding S. 1383, the SAVE America Act, with amendments focused on voting rights and election protections, and voted on confirming Colin McDonald of California as an Assistant Attorney General. Multiple Senate and House committees were scheduled throughout the week to address topics ranging from national security and arms control to voting rights, election infrastructure, export financing, telecommunications, and energy security.

Amendment SA 4753, submitted by Senators Durbin and Warnock, proposes comprehensive revisions to the Voting Rights Act, rebranding it as the John R. Lewis Voting Rights Advancement Act of 2026. The amendment expands voting protections by establishing new standards for proving vote dilution and denial claims based on totality of circumstances, including social and historical discrimination. It replaces previous coverage formulas with a system triggered when voting rights violations occur within a 25-year period and creates practice-based preclearance for covered voting changes when jurisdictions meet specific demographic thresholds.

The amendment strengthens enforcement mechanisms by expanding the Attorney General's authority to investigate voting practices, demand production of voting-related materials, and assign election observers. It provides new protections for election workers and poll watchers through criminal penalties for intimidation, expands bilingual election requirements through 2037, and includes a companion measure criminalizing interference with voters' ability to vote and protecting election infrastructure. Senator Bennet simultaneously submitted amendments addressing tariff rates on infant and early childhood products to maintain normal trade relations duties at January 19, 2025 levels.

Mullin's confirmation occurred amid significant partisan disagreement over both his nomination and broader departmental issues. Though receiving bipartisan personal praise for his character and work ethic, some Democrats expressed concerns about his management experience and the department's conduct
