# Congressional Digest Summary: March 11, 2026

The Senate met on March 11, 2026, and continued consideration of H.R. 6644, the Housing for the 21st Century Act, advancing amendments and voting to proceed with post-cloture debate scheduled for Thursday, March 12, at 11:30 a.m.

Senator Grassley spoke in recognition of the 40th anniversary of National TRIO Day, highlighting how the six-program initiative supports low-income and first-generation college students, with 54 programs in Iowa serving over 18,000 students in 2025.

The Senate introduced 22 new bills and two resolutions, including Senator Kim's proposal for an annual Senate academic civics competition for high school students and Senator Durbin's comprehensive oversight bill for proprietary higher education institutions. Several bills focused on infrastructure, education, veterans' support, and trade policy.

The Foreign Relations Committee favorably reported eight diplomatic nominees, including ambassadors to Iceland, the Philippines, Slovenia, El Salvador, and New Zealand, along with other State Department positions, all with confirmation recommendations.

Senator Collins introduced legislation to create a commemorative coin program honoring fallen firefighters, noting the upcoming 25th anniversary of September 11 and the sacrifices of first responders throughout American history.

Senators introduced the Pechanga Band of Indians Land Transfer Act, which would transfer 860 acres of federal land in California to tribal trust, enabling better preservation of sacred sites including Pu'eska Mountain.

Senator Schumer criticized the ongoing Iran conflict, noting 8 American servicemembers killed, 140 wounded, and rising inflation already impacting gas prices at $3.58 per gallon. He demanded public Senate hearings with Cabinet officials to explain the administration's rationale for the military operation.

Senator Thune stated that Democrats have abandoned DHS funding negotiations, claiming they are content with current conditions despite a months-long shutdown. He criticized Democratic leadership for "playing politics" rather than engaging in productive compromise on homeland security reforms.

Multiple Senate committees held significant hearings including the Environment and Public Works Committee on cold-weather diesel engine reliability, the Finance Subcommittee on the fiscal outlook through 2036, the Indian Affairs Committee on water rights settlements with Western tribes, and the Special Committee
