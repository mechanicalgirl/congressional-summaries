# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-06-19)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-102/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-102/daily-digest)

On June 17, 2026, the Senate confirmed Michelle Steel as Ambassador to the Republic of Korea and advanced George Holding's nomination for U.S. Director of the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development. The chamber passed legislation protecting the Ocean Observatories Initiative from defunding and resolutions honoring the New York Knicks' NBA championship and recognizing U.S. Foreign Service Day. Senators also agreed to resume consideration of the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act with a cloture vote scheduled for the following day.

Senate committees advanced numerous nominations and legislative initiatives across multiple policy areas. The Commerce, Science, and Transportation Committee moved forward on student athlete rights protections, while the Foreign Relations Committee reported favorably on international measures addressing Sudan's stability, Arctic security, and combating Chinese criminal organizations in Latin America. The Judiciary Committee considered federal court media coverage legislation and judicial nominations, and the Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions Committee advanced healthcare reauthorization bills.

Multiple senators submitted amendments to the fiscal year 2027 Defense Authorization bill covering diverse defense and military concerns. Senator Kim's proposals included a pilot program for integrating unmanned aircraft systems with munitions, a feedback platform modeled on Ukraine's BRAVE-1 system to improve military acquisition decisions, expanded quantum information science research, and a Defense Digital Engineering Center of Excellence. Senator Cruz submitted amendments protecting Expeditionary Combat Aviation Brigades from retirement, establishing trademark protections for military awards, allowing service members' children to maintain homeschooling across state lines, and conditioning military engagement with Mexico on water treaty compliance.

Senator Murphy of Connecticut delivered sharp criticism of a recently announced war settlement with Iran, denouncing it as a humiliating defeat that left the United States significantly weakened. Murphy contended that Iran made no new commitments while the United States lifted oil sanctions, unfroze billions in assets, and established a $300 billion reparations fund, eliminating all leverage for future nuclear negotiations. He characterized the conflict as the greatest foreign policy blunder in twenty years, noting that despite 100 days of war and American casualties, Iran retained its nuclear program, missile capabilities, and terrorist support networks while the United States emerged in a fundamentally compromised position.
