# Congressional Record Vol. 172, No. 46 Summary

## Senate Activities

The Senate passed the Housing for the 21st Century Act (H.R. 6644) by a vote of 89-10, aiming to increase the supply of housing in America. Additionally, the Senate agreed to S. Res. 642, celebrating the YMCA's 175th anniversary of service to American communities. The chamber also considered the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act but rejected a cloture motion on the motion to proceed to consideration of H.R. 7147 by a vote of 51-46. The Senate scheduled a cloture vote on the Anna St. John judicial nomination for Monday, March 16, 2026, at 5:30 p.m.

## Nominations and Withdrawals

The Senate received two new nominations, including Sarah B. Rogers to be CEO of the United States Agency for Global Media and Army Brigadier General Thad J. Collard for promotion to major general. Meanwhile, the administration withdrew the nominations of Jeremy Carl for Assistant Secretary of State and William Hewes III for the Consumer Product Safety Commission.

## Senate Committee Activities

The Armed Services Committee examined the posture of U.S. European Command and U.S. Transportation Command in review of the Defense Authorization Request for Fiscal Year 2027. The Commerce, Science, and Transportation Committee favorably reported several nominations, including Matthew Anderson for Deputy NASA Administrator and Seval Oz for Assistant Secretary of Transportation. The Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions Committee heard testimony on foreign influence in higher education, while the Judiciary Committee reported two nominees for U.S. Attorney positions.

## House Legislative Activity

The House introduced 44 public bills (H.R. 7890-7933) and 6 resolutions. Three bills received committee reports: H.R. 7640 addressing immigration enforcement, H.R. 2709 concerning giant sequoia health, and H.R. 6380 establishing Chiricahua National Park in Arizona.

## Bills Introduced

Among the new legislation, Representative Houchin introduced H.R. 7890 to improve literacy instruction based on reading science, and Representative Davidson introduced H.R. 7901 on foreign intelligence
