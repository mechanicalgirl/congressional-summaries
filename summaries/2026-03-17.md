# Congressional Record Summary: March 12, 2026

## Senate Activity

The Senate passed the Housing for the 21st Century Act (H.R. 6644) by a vote of 89-10, legislation aimed at increasing housing supply across America. Additionally, the chamber approved S. Res. 642, recognizing the YMCA's 175th anniversary and celebrating its role in community service.

Regarding the controversial Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act (H.R. 7147), the Senate rejected a cloture motion with a 51-46 vote, falling short of the required three-fifths majority needed to limit debate. Senator Thune entered a motion to reconsider this vote.

The Senate began consideration of Anna St. John's nomination to be a United States District Judge for the Eastern District of Louisiana. A cloture motion was filed, with the vote scheduled for 5:30 p.m. on Monday, March 16, 2026.

## Committee Actions

The Committee on Armed Services held hearings examining the posture of U.S. European Command and U.S. Transportation Command. The Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation favorably reported five nominations, including Matthew Anderson for Deputy NASA Administrator and Seval Oz for Assistant Secretary of Transportation, plus routine Coast Guard appointments.

The Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions held a hearing on foreign influence in higher education, featuring testimony from representatives of the National Association of Scholars, Foundation for Defense of Democracies, and The Asia Society. The Committee on the Judiciary favorably reported two U.S. Attorney nominations.

## House Activity

The House introduced 44 public bills (H.R. 7890-7933) and 6 resolutions, covering diverse topics including education reform, higher education identity fraud prevention, healthcare provisions, and other policy matters. Three committees filed reports on bills addressing immigration enforcement, giant sequoia health, and establishing Chiricahua National Park.

The House convened at 9:30 a.m. for a brief session, with Pastor Zack Randles of Waterfront Church DC delivering the opening prayer, and adjourned at 9:38 a.m. No recorded votes were taken during the session.
