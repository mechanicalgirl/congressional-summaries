# Congressional Record Volume 172, No. 46 - Summary

## Senate Activity (March 12, 2026)

The Senate passed the Housing for the 21st Century Act (H.R. 6644) by a vote of 89-10, aimed at increasing housing supply nationwide. The chamber also agreed to Senate Resolution 642, celebrating the YMCA's 175th anniversary. A motion to proceed to the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act (H.R. 7147) failed to achieve the required three-fifths majority needed for cloture, with the vote at 51-46.

## Judicial Nomination Progress

The Senate began consideration of Anna St. John's nomination to be a U.S. District Judge for the Eastern District of Louisiana. A cloture motion was entered, with the vote scheduled for 5:30 p.m. on Monday, March 16, 2026. This reflects the Senate's ongoing confirmation process for federal judiciary positions.

## Committee Actions

The Senate Committee on Armed Services held open and closed hearings examining the posture of U.S. European Command and U.S. Transportation Command. The Commerce Committee favorably reported several nominations, including candidates for NASA Deputy Administrator and the National Transportation Safety Board. The Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions Committee examined foreign influence in higher education, while the Judiciary Committee advanced two U.S. Attorney nominations.

## New Legislation Introduced

Both chambers introduced significant new bills. The Senate saw 29 bills and four resolutions introduced, while the House introduced 44 public bills and six resolutions. Notable Senate proposals include the ACTION for National Service Act (expanding AmeriCorps opportunities) and the No Crypto in Social Security Act (prohibiting cryptocurrency investments by Social Security Trust Funds).

## House Floor Activity

The House convened briefly on March 12, introducing bills and referring them to appropriate committees before adjourning. Notable House bills address education, healthcare, immigration enforcement, and veterans' benefits. Three committee reports were filed covering immigration enforcement, giant sequoia conservation, and establishment of Chiricahua National Park.
