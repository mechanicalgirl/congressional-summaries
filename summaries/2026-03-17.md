# Congressional Record Summary - March 12, 2026

**Senate Legislative Activity**

The Senate passed the Housing for the 21st Century Act (H.R. 6644) by a vote of 89-10, aimed at increasing housing supply across America. The chamber also agreed to S. Res. 642, celebrating the Young Men's Christian Association's 175th anniversary of service to American communities.

**Judicial Nomination Progress**

The Senate began consideration of Anna St. John's nomination to serve as United States District Judge for the Eastern District of Louisiana. A cloture vote on this nomination is scheduled for 5:30 p.m. on Monday, March 16, 2026. St. John's confirmation debate represents ongoing Senate focus on judicial appointments.

**Executive Committee Work**

Multiple Senate committees held hearings and business meetings. The Armed Services Committee heard testimony on U.S. European Command and Transportation Command posture for fiscal year 2027. The Commerce Committee favorably reported five nominations, including positions at NASA and the Transportation Safety Board. The Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions Committee examined foreign influence in higher education institutions. The Judiciary Committee advanced two U.S. Attorney nominations.

**DHS Appropriations Debate**

The Senate rejected a cloture motion on the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act (H.R. 7147) with 51 votes in favor versus 46 needed. Senate Majority Leader Thune entered a motion to reconsider this procedural vote.

**New Legislation Introduced**

The Senate introduced 29 bills and four resolutions. Notable proposals include the ACTION for National Service Act, which would expand AmeriCorps to one million positions within a decade and increase education benefits, and the No Crypto in Social Security Act, prohibiting cryptocurrency investments by Social Security Trust Funds.

**House Legislative Activity**

The House introduced 44 public bills (H.R. 7890-7933) and six resolutions covering diverse topics including education, healthcare, immigration, and veterans' benefits. Three bills received committee reports and were prepared for floor consideration.
