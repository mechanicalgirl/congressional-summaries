# Congressional Digest Summary: March 12, 2026

## Senate Legislative Actions

The Senate passed the Housing for the 21st Century Act (H.R. 6644) with an 89-10 vote, aimed at increasing housing supply across America. The chamber also approved a resolution celebrating the YMCA's 175th anniversary. Two record votes were taken, and the Senate considered the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act, though a cloture motion failed 51-46, falling short of the required three-fifths threshold.

## Senate Nominations and Confirmations

The Commerce Committee favorably reported several nominations, including Matthew Anderson for NASA Deputy Administrator, Michael Graham for the National Transportation Safety Board, and other executive positions. The Judiciary Committee advanced nominations for two U.S. Attorneys: Melissa Holyoak for Utah and Phillip Williams Jr. for Northern Alabama. Two nominations were withdrawn: Jeremy Carl for Assistant Secretary of State and William Hewes III for the Consumer Product Safety Commission.

## Upcoming Senate Business

The Senate will resume consideration of Anna St. John's nomination to be a U.S. District Judge for the Eastern District of Louisiana on Monday, March 16 at 3 p.m., with a cloture vote scheduled for 5:30 p.m. The chamber adjourned at 4:24 p.m. Thursday, convening again Monday afternoon.

## House Legislative Activity

The House introduced 44 public bills (H.R. 7890-7933) and 6 resolutions during the session. Three committee reports were filed, including legislation on immigration enforcement, giant sequoia health, and the establishment of Chiricahua National Park. The House convened briefly at 9:30 a.m. and adjourned at 9:38 a.m., with Representative Wittman serving as Speaker pro tempore.

## House Committee Meetings

No House committee hearings were held on the legislative day. A joint Commission on Security and Cooperation in Europe briefing on nuclear power and lessons from Ukraine was scheduled for Monday morning at 10:30 a.m.
