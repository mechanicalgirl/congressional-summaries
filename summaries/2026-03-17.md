# Congressional Record Daily Digest Summary - March 12, 2026

## Senate Legislative Activity

The Senate passed the Housing for the 21st Century Act (H.R. 6644) by a vote of 89-10, addressing housing supply challenges across America. Additionally, the Senate unanimously approved S. Res. 642, recognizing the YMCA's 175th anniversary and its contributions to American communities through youth programs, fitness initiatives, and community services. The Senate also introduced resolutions supporting International Women's Day and recognizing Girl Scouts' 114th birthday.

## Judicial and Executive Nominations

The Senate initiated consideration of Anna St. John's nomination for United States District Judge for the Eastern District of Louisiana, with a cloture vote scheduled for Monday, March 16, at 5:30 p.m. The chamber received new nominations for Cabinet positions, including Sarah B. Rogers as CEO of the United States Agency for Global Media, and withdrew two previous nominations: Jeremy Carl for Assistant Secretary of State and William Hewes III for Consumer Product Safety Commissioner.

## House Legislative Activity

The House introduced 44 public bills and 6 resolutions during the day's brief session, covering topics from education and healthcare to infrastructure and national parks. Three bills received committee reports: H.R. 7640 on immigration enforcement, H.R. 2709 on giant sequoia conservation, and H.R. 6380 establishing Chiricahua National Park. The House met for only eight minutes, adjourning until Monday.

## Committee Actions and Hearings

The Senate Armed Services Committee conducted hearings on U.S. European Command and Transportation Command posture, examining defense authorization requests for Fiscal Year 2027. The Commerce Committee favorably reported several executive nominations including appointments to the NASA, National Transportation Safety Board, and Department of Commerce. The Health Committee examined foreign influence in higher education, while the Judiciary Committee approved nominations for U.S. Attorneys.
