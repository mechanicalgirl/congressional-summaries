# Congressional Digest Summary: March 12, 2026

## Senate Activities and Legislation

The Senate passed the Housing for the 21st Century Act (H.R. 6644) with a strong bipartisan vote of 89-10, aimed at increasing the nation's housing supply. The chamber also unanimously approved a resolution celebrating the Young Men's Christian Association's 175th anniversary, recognizing its contributions to community development and civic engagement across America.

## Judicial Nomination Process

The Senate advanced consideration of Anna St. John's nomination as United States District Judge for the Eastern District of Louisiana. A cloture motion was filed with voting scheduled for Monday, March 16, at 5:30 p.m., following debate on the nomination which began Thursday afternoon.

## Appropriations Debate Stalls

The Senate rejected a motion to advance the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act (H.R. 7147) by a vote of 51-46, failing to achieve the required 60-vote threshold for cloture. The Majority Leader subsequently entered a motion to reconsider this vote.

## Committee Actions and Confirmations

The Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation ordered favorable reports on five nominations, including Matthew Anderson for NASA Deputy Administrator, Michael Graham for the National Transportation Safety Board, and other executive positions. The Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions held hearings examining foreign influence in higher education institutions.

## House Legislative Activity

The House introduced 44 public bills (H.R. 7890-7933) and 6 resolutions during its abbreviated session, which lasted only eight minutes. Three bills received committee reports: one on immigration enforcement, one on giant sequoia conservation, and one establishing Chiricahua National Park in Arizona.

## Bills and Policy Initiatives

New legislation included measures addressing federal student aid fraud detection, higher education improvements, workplace protections, environmental conservation, and healthcare access expansion. Notable proposals included efforts to improve literacy instruction, Medicare eligibility for ages 50-64, and expanded AmeriCorps service opportunities.
