# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-03-28)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-57/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-57/daily-digest)

On March 26, 2026, Congress conducted extensive legislative business across both chambers. The Senate passed the Homeland Security and Further Additional Continuing Appropriations Act and advanced two judicial nominations while establishing pro forma sessions through mid-April and introducing numerous bills and resolutions. The House passed H.R. 8029, the Pay Our Homeland Defenders Act, providing approximately $97 billion in appropriations for the Department of Homeland Security for fiscal year 2026, and agreed to H. Res. 1128 expressing support for the agency. Committees conducted extensive hearings and markups covering topics ranging from Arctic security and education espionage to flood insurance, veterans' affairs, and child welfare funding.

Members of Congress held a Special Order hour addressing immigration enforcement practices and conditions at detention facilities like the Dilley Family Detention Center in Texas. Democratic speakers expressed concern about inadequate food and water, insufficient medical care, and psychological trauma inflicted on children, many of whom were detained far longer than the legally permitted 20 days during routine activities like medical visits and school drop-offs. They called for ending family detention, defunding Immigration and Customs Enforcement, and implementing policies allowing families to navigate immigration cases together in their communities, arguing that protecting children should transcend partisan politics.

The House debate over H.R. 8029 and H. Res. 1128 revealed significant partisan divisions over the 41-day shutdown of DHS, the longest partial shutdown in U.S. history that began February 14, 2026. Republicans argued that Democrats were obstructing full DHS funding to pressure policy changes on immigration enforcement and emphasized hardship faced by unpaid federal workers like TSA screeners. Democrats countered that the administration was using the shutdown as leverage and proposed alternative legislation to separately fund non-controversial DHS agencies like TSA and FEMA while continuing negotiations on ICE and Customs and Border Protection policy reforms including warrant requirements and body camera mandates.

Senator Thune's Amendment 4790 to H.R. 7147 established comprehensive funding for the Department of Homeland Security across five major categories totaling over $34 billion, with additional allocations for the Supreme Court and Federal Aviation Administration. Senator Warnock submitted significant amendments addressing affordable housing through the Downpayment Toward Equity Act of 2026
