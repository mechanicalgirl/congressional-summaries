# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-05-17)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-83/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-83/daily-digest)

The House passed H.R. 8469, the Military Construction, Veterans Affairs, and Related Agencies Appropriations Act for fiscal year 2027, by a vote of 400-15 on May 15, 2026. The bill included amendments that increased funding for stellate ganglion block therapy to treat PTSD in veterans and prohibited the closure of a VA clinic in Port Charlotte, Florida. However, an amendment proposing to redirect nearly $482 million from the NATO Security Investment Program to domestic military construction was rejected during consideration.

The Senate adjourned on May 15 and is scheduled to resume on May 18 at 3 p.m. to continue consideration of en bloc nominations, with a confirmation vote set for 5:30 p.m. The House will reconvene on May 19 at noon. During this period, various congressional members submitted remarks for the record addressing topics including cryptocurrency regulation, the importance of cash payments, recognition of police and military service, and local community accomplishments.
