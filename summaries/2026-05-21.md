# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-05-21)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-85/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-85/daily-digest)

The Senate convened on May 19, 2026, passing three resolutions including recognition of Jewish American Heritage Month and honoring late Senator John Seymour of California, while advancing procedural motions on several judicial nominees. The chamber confirmed United States District Judge Sheria Akins Clarke of South Carolina by a 52-38 vote, confirmed Navy Admiral Douglas L. Williams for appointment as Vice Admiral, and confirmed Judge Evan Rikhye to the U.S. District Court of the Virgin Islands. In parallel committee activity, Senate panels conducted extensive budget hearings across major departments including Justice, Labor, Defense, and Transportation for fiscal year 2027.

The House considered eight measures under suspension of rules focusing primarily on veterans benefits and services, including the Fisher House Availability Act and the Veterans Community Care Scheduling Improvement Act. The chamber also passed commemorative legislation including the 25th Anniversary of 9/11 Commemorative Coin Act, with surcharges supporting the National September 11 Memorial and Museum, alongside bills addressing foster youth housing and North Dakota trust lands. Congress passed the Fostering the Future Act, the first comprehensive modernization of the John H. Chafee Foster Care Program since 1999, expanding education and training opportunities for foster youth aging out of the system by increasing education vouchers from $5,000 to $12,000.

Congress advanced multiple bills addressing workforce challenges and land management, including the Veterans' Transition to Trucking Act allowing the VA to approve multi-state apprenticeship programs for commercial truck driving schools to address driver shortages, and the North Dakota Trust Lands Completion Act facilitating land exchanges for tribal reservation consolidation. The Senate also approved amendments to Senate Procurement Regulations streamlining contracting processes while maintaining competitive bidding requirements, clarifying approval thresholds and establishing procedures for various purchase categories.

The 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act represents comprehensive legislation addressing housing affordability and supply challenges through multiple coordinated programs. The legislation establishes enhanced housing counseling programs, federal guidelines for multifamily housing, exemptions from environmental reviews for infill construction, a database of publicly owned land, and updated zoning framework guidelines. It further expands housing opportunities through priority funding for opportunity zones, whole-home repair pilots, streamlined environmental reviews, and updated FHA mortgage limits reflecting current construction costs.
