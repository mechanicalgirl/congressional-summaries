# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-05-13)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-79/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-79/daily-digest)

During the second session of the 119th Congress from January through April 2026, both chambers demonstrated significant legislative activity with the Senate convening for 69 days and the House for 62 days, passing 131 and 197 measures respectively, resulting in 20 total enacted public bills. The Senate confirmed 3,147 executive nominations while leaving 1,055 unconfirmed, and together Congress introduced 3,199 measures during this period, with 282 measures pending in the Senate and 108 in the House. For the week of May 12-15, 2026, the Senate scheduled a confirmation vote on Kevin Warsh for the Federal Reserve Board, while multiple appropriations subcommittees prepared budget hearings for fiscal year 2027 funding requests across agencies including Defense, the FBI, the EPA, and HUD, and the House planned to consider various measures on financial services, including artificial intelligence and cryptocurrency regulation.

During National Police Week, partisan tensions emerged as Republican leadership advocated for tens of billions in Department of Homeland Security funding for Immigration and Customs Enforcement and Border Patrol, while Democratic leadership opposed a $72 billion reconciliation bill that included a controversial $1 billion White House ballroom renovation. Multiple senators paid tribute to fallen law enforcement officers, including four Ohio officers killed in 2025 and Chicago police officer John Bartholomew, while some expressed concern about judicial nominees minimizing the January 6 Capitol attack. In related developments, Senate Majority Leader Schumer characterized Iranian hostilities as an undeclared war exceeding the War Powers Act threshold and warned of rising gas prices, while also criticizing the Trump administration for firing public health workers during a hantavirus outbreak aboard a cruise ship despite associated cost savings.
