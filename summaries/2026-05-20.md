# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-05-20)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-84/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-84/daily-digest)

The Senate convened on Monday, May 18, 2026, and confirmed 47 executive branch nominations by a vote of 46-43, including United States Attorneys, Marshals, Assistant Secretaries across multiple departments, and various agency officials and ambassadors. The Senate additionally received 147 military nominations for officers in the Air Force, Army, and Navy to be appointed or promoted to general and admiral ranks. Ten new bills were introduced covering topics such as maternal health data collection, overtime compensation requirements, and digital advertising restrictions.

The Senate agreed to a unanimous consent agreement scheduling Tuesday's proceedings, which will include cloture and confirmation votes for Sheria Akins Clarke's nomination for United States District Judge for South Carolina and Evan Rikhye's nomination for the District Court of the Virgin Islands, along with a vote on S.J. Res. 185 concerning hostilities with Iran. The week ahead features extensive committee activity across both chambers, with the Senate Appropriations Committee holding budget hearings, the Armed Services Committee examining military posture and defense requests, and various other committees addressing healthcare, energy policy, foreign relations, and veterans affairs.

The House of Representatives passed three bills addressing bail reform and court procedures, including measures to create a public list of cashless bail jurisdictions, prohibit bail-related fraud, and establish conditions on court-appointed monitors. The House made several appointments, including Asif Mahmood of California to the Commission on International Religious Freedom and four individuals to the House Child Care Center Advisory Board. Meanwhile, the Senate confirmed 49 executive nominations including U.S. Attorneys, ambassadors, and assistant secretaries across multiple federal departments.
