# Summary of Congressional Digest - March 5, 2026

## Senate Activity

The Senate continued consideration of H.R. 6644, the Housing for the 21st Century Act, with multiple amendments pending debate. A cloture motion was filed on the Scott/Warren Amendment No. 4308, with a vote scheduled for Monday, March 9 at 5:30 p.m. The Senate passed two measures: S. 836, the Children and Teens' Online Privacy Protection Act, and S. 2074, the Servicemembers' Credit Monitoring Enhancement Act. Twenty-seven bills and nine resolutions were introduced during the session.

## House Legislative Action

The House approved the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act (H.R. 7744) by a vote of 221-209, rejecting a motion to recommit by a margin of 212-217. The chamber also passed H. Res. 1099, reaffirming Iran as the largest state sponsor of terrorism with a 372-53 vote. However, H. Con. Res. 38, directing removal of U.S. Armed Forces from Iran, failed 212-219. The House introduced 49 public bills and 4 resolutions.

## Committee Activity

Multiple House committees held significant hearings and markups. The Agriculture Committee advanced the Farm, Food, and National Security Act. The Energy and Commerce Committee marked up nine bills focusing on child care fraud prevention and internet safety measures. The Judiciary Committee reported favorably on S. 3798 regarding ATM violations and several judicial nominations. The Education and Workforce Committee approved eight bills addressing child care fraud and accountability.

## Nominations and Judicial Confirmations

The Senate Judiciary Committee advanced nominations for Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives Director Robert Cekada and several federal judges for Western District of Texas, Eastern District of Louisiana, and Western District of Arkansas positions. Additional U.S. Marshal nominations for Iowa, South Dakota, and Maine were also reported favorably.
