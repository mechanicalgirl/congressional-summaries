# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-07-04)

[https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-110/daily-digest](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-172/issue-110/daily-digest)

Both chambers of Congress held brief pro forma sessions on Thursday, July 2, 2026, during the nation's Independence Day recess as Americans prepared to celebrate the country's 250th anniversary. The Senate convened for approximately 28 seconds while the House met for just over two minutes, with neither chamber conducting any votes or substantive legislative business. Both bodies are scheduled to reconvene in pro forma session the following Monday, July 6, with the Senate meeting at 9 a.m. and the House at 10:30 a.m.

Despite the abbreviated nature of the sessions, the House introduced 21 public bills and 9 resolutions and processed 13 committee reports addressing healthcare, education, and workforce development matters. Members used the occasion to place remarks in the Congressional Record commemorating America's semiquincentennial anniversary and honoring various individuals and organizations. These tributes recognized distinguished educators and military officers as well as community organizations, including Dr. Chace Anderson's 43-year education career and the Neighborhood Partnership Housing Services' 35 years of community development work in California.
