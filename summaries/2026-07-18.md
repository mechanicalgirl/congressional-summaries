# Congressional Summary (run on 2026-07-18)

On Thursday, July 16, 2026, the Senate confirmed multiple judicial and trade nominees and approved 160 military nominees while beginning debate on a Sixth Circuit Court of Appeals nomination, with a cloture vote scheduled for Monday. The House advanced the Take Care of America's Veterans Act by narrowly rejecting a motion to recommit the bill 211 to 210, and unanimously approved legislation naming a Veterans Affairs clinic in Marietta, Georgia after Colonel Michael H. Boyce. Additionally, the Senate voted against proceeding with a resolution disapproving a Medicare prior authorization rule, and the Budget Committee ordered favorable reporting of the fiscal year 2027 budget resolution.

The Take Care of America's Veterans Act represents comprehensive legislation addressing veteran benefits and Department of Veterans Affairs operations across multiple titles. The bill includes the Major Richard Star Act, which expands concurrent receipt of military retirement pay and VA disability compensation for combat-related injuries, and the Love Lives On Act, allowing surviving spouses to retain benefits upon remarriage. It increases supplemental allowances for veterans receiving aid and attendance, implements disability rating reforms for specific conditions, and establishes measures to improve claims processing efficiency. Title II focuses on educational and economic opportunities, including modifications to the Post-9/11 GI Bill, expanded eligibility for housing loans to Reserve and National Guard members, and enhanced transition assistance programs requiring coordination between the Departments of Veterans Affairs, Defense, and Labor.

The legislation establishes significant modifications to veterans' mental health research and suicide prevention initiatives while addressing blast exposure-related injuries. The Precision Medicine for Veterans Initiative is expanded to include research on repetitive low-level blast exposure and traumatic brain injury, with new data-sharing partnerships between the VA and Department of Defense to consolidate research efforts. A new Blast Overpressure Task Force is created to improve healthcare for veterans with blast-related injuries, and suicide prevention oversight is strengthened by requiring military occupational data in national reports. The bill also extends VA-DOD healthcare resource sharing agreements and mandates audits assessing coordination effectiveness.

The legislation establishes comprehensive reforms to the Veterans Community Care Program and creates new mental health residential treatment initiatives, requiring standardized screening processes and establishing priority admission criteria based on clinical need.
