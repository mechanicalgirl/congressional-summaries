# Summary of Congressional Record Volume 172, No. 38

## Senate Proceedings (February 26, 2026)

The Senate convened at 10 a.m. and passed several measures including two bills related to Native Alaskan land entitlements and a Nevada water pipeline project. Senators debated the Housing for the 21st Century Act (H.R. 6644), with a cloture vote scheduled for Monday, March 2 at 5:30 p.m. The chamber confirmed Ryan McCormack of Virginia as Under Secretary of Transportation for Policy by a vote of 57-33.

## Legislative Actions

Six resolutions were passed by unanimous consent, designating various observances including Career and Technical Education Month, National STEM Week, National Public Schools Week, Rare Disease Day, National Elizabeth Peratrovich Day, and National FFA Week. The Senate also introduced 30 bills covering diverse topics such as medical nutrition therapy for Medicare beneficiaries, renewable energy standards, and tribal firearms access.

## Government Funding Crisis

Senator Lankford criticized the ongoing partial Department of Homeland Security shutdown, noting that 260,000 federal employees are working without pay. He argued that cutting DHS funding affects TSA, Coast Guard, FEMA, and cybersecurity agencies beyond immigration enforcement, which Democrats allegedly seek to defund. He proposed legislation ensuring automatic continuing resolutions during budget negotiations to prevent employee salary interruptions.

## Iran Tensions and War Powers

Senator Kaine discussed pending War Powers Resolution regarding potential Iran conflict. He detailed the historical U.S.-Iran relationship, including the 1953 CIA-backed coup, U.S. funding of Iraq during the Iran-Iraq War, and the 2015 nuclear agreement's subsequent abandonment. Kaine argued that war is unnecessary and dangerous, noting the USS Ford carrier deployment to the Middle East is now 11 months—the longest since Vietnam—straining sailors and ship maintenance.

## Key Senate Speeches

Senator Banks praised the State of the Union Address, highlighting improvements in border security, inflation reduction, and stock market gains under President Trump. Senator Schumer criticized the administration's handling of Epstein files, tariff impacts on families, and manufacturing job losses, while outlining Democratic initiatives to reduce food and living costs.
